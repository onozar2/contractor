## 1. Executive Summary — the five decisions that matter

1. **Do not "buy leads" as your primary strategy. Own the demand.** Shared-marketplace leads (Angi/HomeAdvisor) get split 3–8 ways — worth flagging rhetorically as ~$200–250 "per real shot" on a $50 lead — but the number that matters is **cost per signed job: $600–1,000+ for an established shop, worse for a zero-review startup** ([leadtruffle.co](https://www.leadtruffle.co/blog/angi-leads-cost-pricing-contractors-2026/) — *vendor-blog benchmark, treat as [EST]-grade*). The escape hatch is owning the funnel: Google Business Profile + reviews, jobsite-radius canvassing, referrals, and your own ads→landing-page→booking machine. That is exactly what We The People Construction (WTP) does.

2. **The first 6 months are carried by offline + free channels, not SEO or paid ads.** Local SEO takes 2–4 months for map-pack movement and 6–9 months for content ([seo.com](https://www.seo.com/blog/how-long-does-seo-take/)). Only three things produce appointments in *week one*: jobsite-radius door-knocking/hangers, realtor/referral outreach, and Nextdoor posts. **Paid digital contributes well under one signed job/month at zero reviews (§6) — canvassing and referrals are the months-1–3 engine.**

3. **Stand up Google Business Profile + a review-ask flow on day one — it's free and the highest-leverage asset you have.** The Map Pack captures ~42% of local-search clicks ([minyona.com](https://minyona.com/blog/google-business-profile-contractors) — *vendor-blog benchmark, treat as [EST]-grade; published map-pack click-share studies range ~33–44%*); GBP is the one platform where soliciting reviews is *encouraged* (Yelp bans it — §4). Reviews accrue from jobs closed through *any* channel, including door-knocks, so this compounds regardless of source — and it is what unlocks LSA lead *volume* (§4).

4. **Build your own "mini-NCT," don't rent NCT.** NCT Media's machine (ads → questionnaire landing page → call-center screening → calendar booking) is not proprietary — Joon already has the dev capability and a Mongo CRM. The only piece NCT sells that Joon lacks is *call-center labor* (speed-to-lead answering). That's a staffing problem, not a software one. Buy one small *capped test* from Hybrid Reach or NCT purely to benchmark real LA cost-per-booked-job while you build your own funnel.

5. **Track cost-per-SIGNED-JOB, not cost-per-lead — from the very first dollar.** Ori's "$200–300/lead" belief is directionally right for *shared* leads but conflates three different numbers (§2). Install the tracking stack (GA4 + one call-tracking number per channel + CRM source fields + weekly scorecard) *before* the first door hanger goes out. The garage-door-company case study that nearly wrote off $187K of door-hanger revenue as "not working" — until they measured it with QR+UTM — is the exact failure to avoid ([pipelineon.com](https://pipelineon.com/blog/contractor-conversion-rate-optimization/)).

**The go/no-go number (§6 cash summary):** first-90-day cash outlay ≈ **$13.7–17.2K**; full-year all-in ≈ **~$127–131K [EST]** (marketing $87K + one-times $4.5–8.2K + field-sales labor ~$36K); **max cash Frank must be prepared to float before job collections catch up ≈ $42–50K through month 6.**

## 2. How the lead market actually works

### The three numbers Ori is conflating
Every "lead cost" conversation must separate:

1. **Sticker CPL** — what the platform bills per lead.
2. **"Per real shot" (rhetoric only, NOT a math input)** — sticker CPL × how many contractors a shared lead is sold to. A $50 Angi lead split 4–5 ways ≈ **$200–250 per real shot** ([leadtruffle.co](https://www.leadtruffle.co/blog/angi-leads-cost-pricing-contractors-2026/) — *vendor-blog benchmark, treat as [EST]-grade*). This is a useful gut-check on *why* shared leads feel expensive — but do **not** feed it into the signed-job formula.
3. **Cost per signed job = sticker CPL ÷ your win rate on that channel's leads.** The win rate already prices in shared-lead competition, so this is the single, non-double-counted number. Worked example: Angi at **$50–100 sticker ÷ a 6–15% win rate = $600–1,000/signed job** — which is exactly the leadtruffle figure. (The old formula multiplied by share-count *and* divided by a close rate that already reflects sharing; that double-penalized and produced an impossible $2,900–8,300. Dropped.)

**Per-channel planning win rates used throughout:**
- **Shared marketplace (Angi):** 6–15% win rate → $600–1,000/signed job.
- **Exclusive high-intent (LSA):** 8–15% for a zero-review GC [EST] → **~$700–2,000/signed job** at $75–210 CPL. **Planning band is 8–15% [EST] at cold start; it is revised upward to 12–18% (mo 4–6) and 15–20% (mo 7–12) in the §6 funnels on the explicit assumption of 20+ accrued reviews and sub-5-minute response speed by month 7 — revert to 8–15% if reviews lag.**
- **Google Search (exclusive, tight-targeted):** 5–10% → **$2,100–5,000/signed job** at $150–400 CPL (higher without an offer, lower with the free-3D-model hook).
- **Meta (lower intent):** 4–8% → high per-job cost until retargeting + reviews mature.

### Verdict on "leads cost $200–300 each that you bid on"
**Partially right, mislabeled.** Sticker CPL on shared platforms is actually *cheaper* than he thinks ($15–100 for remodeling — [improveandgrow.com](https://improveandgrow.com/contractors-and-trades/is-angi-worth-it-for-contractors/)), but the *per-real-shot* framing lands in his $200–300 range because leads are shared 3–8 ways. On the exclusive channels he's picturing (Search, LSA), real planning CPLs are:
- **LSA (General Contractor), LA:** avg $112.95/lead, median $74.77, range $39–$210 ([99calls](https://99calls.com/LSA-Cost-Estimator/general-contractor)); plan $75–$210 given LA competition.
- **Google Search, kitchen/bath remodel CPL:** $150–$400; **ADU-qualified lead** $250–$600 against $250K–$400K contract values ([Kitchen Remodeling SEO](https://kitchenremodelingseo.com/what-kitchen-remodeling-leads-cost-2026/), [BoxBuild](https://boxbuild.agency/google-ads-adu-lead-growth/)).
- **Meta:** $80–$150/lead in LA [EST band from AZ data — [M.Wolf Media](https://mwolfmedia.com/how-much-do-facebook-ads-cost-for-contractors/)], lower intent (4–8% close).

**The correction that matters:** optimize **cost per signed job**, not CPL. A zero-review GC converts *below* platform averages on every paid channel because homeowners filter by reviews first — so real cost-per-signed-job on bought leads will be worse than these benchmarks until the review base exists.

### The structural way to escape high lead costs
You cannot out-bid established competitors on rented leads as a zero-review startup. You **own the demand** on four fronts that compound and don't get split 8 ways:
1. **Reviews (GBP)** — free, and the input to every channel's close rate *and* LSA's lead volume.
2. **Referrals** — **past-client bonus $250–$500 / realtor referral flat $1,000–$2,500 per signed job, tiered by contract size** (see cap in §4). Cost nothing until a job closes ([referralprogrampros.com](https://www.referralprogrampros.com/how-to-pay-a-referral-fee/)).
3. **Jobsite-radius canvassing** — ~$120–270/kept appointment [EST] (§8), pre-warmed by visible proof-of-work next door — the WTP model.
4. **Owned funnel** (your ads→landing→booking) — strips out the vendor markup that exists to pay for call-center labor you can staff cheaper.

Four links, all buildable in-house: **paid ads (FB/IG/Google/YouTube) → branded questionnaire landing page (e.g. nctmediaappointments.com) → in-house call center (7 days/wk, up to 15 hrs/day, outbound call+SMS) → calendar-booked appointment synced to the contractor's CRM.** They run their own top-of-funnel and screening (unlike Angi's shared database), which is what lets them claim exclusivity. ### Design of Joon's own in-house "mini-NCT"
Joon has the dev team and a Mongo CRM — build the machine, keep the margin:

1. **Ads → trade-specific landing page** (not the main site). Short questionnaire: project type, timeline, budget range, homeowner status, zip. Conversion hook = **free in-home estimate + free 3D render** (the WTP differentiator). Joon's dev team can ship this in days.
2. **BathMath-style quote calculator** embedded on the landing page — instant ballpark ("kitchen refresh $38–48K, full remodel $68–110K" — real LA ranges from [firstbuilders.info](https://firstbuilders.info/2026-kitchen-remodeling-costs-in-los-angeles-complete-price-guide-for-homeowners/)) — raises conversion by giving a number before the call.
3. **Speed-to-lead screening** — the real gap NCT sells as labor. Options: (a) a part-time inside-sales person answering within minutes, or (b) an AI voice/SMS qualification bot. 4. **Calendar booking → CRM** — wire a Calendly-style flow into the existing Mongo CRM. Trivial.
5. **Follow-up / no-show reduction** — SMS/email reminders pre-appointment. Standard, cheap.

**Sequencing:** (1) buy one capped test from Hybrid Reach (remodel-native) *now* to get real LA cost-per-booked-job while Frank needs work; (2) in parallel build the in-house funnel; (3) once instrumented (§7), compare blended in-house CAC vs vendor CAC and shift budget to whichever wins per signed job — likely in-house once volume justifies the answering hire.

## 8. Offline & canvassing program

### CA/CSLB compliance checklist — DO THIS BEFORE ANY DOOR-KNOCK
- [ ] **Register every canvasser as a Home Improvement Salesperson (HIS)** with CSLB — required for anyone performing sales duties (price/scope talk or appointment-setting) *outside the fixed place of business*. Door-knockers do **not** qualify for the retail-location exemption. **$200 nonrefundable/person** (confirmed on CSLB's current pre-application page; 50% reduction for qualifying veterans), age 18+, no experience req, associated to Frank's license number ([cslb.ca.gov](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/Contractors/Applicants/Home_Improvement_Registration/Before_Applying_For_HIS.aspx)). Unregistered solicitation is a misdemeanor under **BPC §7153** (verify the specific amending bill against the CSLB page rather than a blog).
- [ ] **HIS registrants must generally be W-2 employees of the licensee** — this drives the canvasser cost math below (payroll tax + CA workers' comp add ~15–25% to the base wage).
- [ ] **Ori registers as HIS too, unless he is added as an officer/qualifier on Frank's license** — the day-1–30 plan has Ori knocking doors, which is HIS-regulated activity. **File day 1; processing runs ~2–4 weeks.**
- [ ] **3-Day Right to Cancel** built into every contract template — any door-knock-solicited deal is a "home solicitation contract" (Civil Code §1689.6/.7): buyer cancels until midnight of the 3rd business day (5th for seniors), 100% refund, **zero compensation for work started**. Use CSLB's required disclosure format. **Do not mobilize crews/materials inside the 3-day window on a solicited deal** ([inhomerenovation.com](https://www.inhomerenovation.com/post/your-california-right-to-cancel-understanding-the-3-day-notice-on-home-improvement-projects)).
- [ ] **LA Municipal Code §41.43.1:** no soliciting 8pm–8am; canvassers carry photo ID + LA Business Tax Registration Certificate, present on request; posted "No Soliciting" = do not knock.
- [ ] **Pull each city's solicitor ordinance before knocking it** — Glendale requires a commercial solicitation license (GMC §9.14.110, 818-937-8300; posted "No Soliciting" is an absolute bar); Burbank requires a separate peddler/solicitor permit (Muni Code Art. 11 — confirm with Community Development). Ordinances are **not uniform** across the Valley/West LA footprint.
- [ ] **Watch AB 1327 (2025–26 session)** — pending changes to home-solicitation cancellation rules; recheck before scaling ([legiscan CA AB1327](https://legiscan.com/CA)).

### Hiring & comp — the first canvasser
- **Hold the hire** until Frank/Ori themselves knock the first jobsite radius and produce a real per-appointment cost benchmark — don't hire on borrowed roofing/solar averages.
- Then post the role (Indeed/ZipRecruiter, SFV/West LA), copy WTP's tone: low barrier, bilingual encouraged, no experience required, criminal-record-inclusive (a real LA labor-pool expander). Dedicated bilingual hiring line, separate from the sales line.
- **Comp: $18–20/hr base + $50–75 per KEPT (sat) appointment** — pay-on-sit, not pay-on-set, because homeowners cancel sets at no cost to the rep [EST]. Market range: base $18–24/hr, bonus $25–100/set-and-kept ([ziprecruiter](https://www.ziprecruiter.com/Jobs/Door-Canvassing/--in-California)).
- **Cost-per-appointment math — show the range, not the best case [EST]:** because HIS canvassers are W-2, load the base with payroll tax + CA workers' comp (~15–25%) → effective **~$22–25/hr**. **These are full ~6-hr knocking days — the day cost that feeds the §6 cash-summary field-labor footnote.**
  - *Best case* (3 kept appts/day): $24×6 + 3×$75 = ~$369/day ÷ 3 = **~$120–145/kept appt**.
  - *Base case* (1–2 kept/day, matching the pace math below): **~$256/day** ÷ 1.5 = **~$170–270/kept appt**. *(This ~$256/day is the number multiplied by knock-days in the §6 cash table: 12 days ≈ $3.1K/mo in mo 4–6, 16–18 days ≈ $4.1–4.6K/mo in mo 7–12.)*
  - The **warm jobsite radius** is what's supposed to pull throughput toward the high-appt end; do not assume best case in the model (§6 uses the 1–2 kept/day base). This range is also the bar the Frank/Ori benchmark knock must beat before hiring.

### Jobsite-radius method (the core tactic)
- Canvass/mail the **50–100 homes within a ~5-min walk** of any active project — neighbors are already curious about the trucks/dumpster/permit board [EST]. Highest-intent, lowest-cost offline tactic.
- Pace: 10–20 doors/hr, 30–40% answer, ~1.5–3% doors-to-appointment cold (higher warm) [EST] — which yields ~1–2 *kept* appts/day after no-shows over a full ~6-hr day, the figure the §6 funnel uses.
- Pair with a **yard-sign / branded permit-board at the jobsite** as the visual trigger before the knock, and a Nextdoor post ("we're doing a kitchen remodel on [Street]") as the digital echo — same message, three surfaces.

### Flyer / door hanger / EDDM specs
- **Door hangers:** $60–200 per 1,000 (4over4/VistaPrint calculators for live quotes). Response 1–3% (home-services 1–2%) [EST]; zero marginal delivery cost when the canvasser hangs them on no-answers.
- **Design:** before/after of a real nearby job ("just finished this kitchen 2 streets over"), neighborhood-specific copy, QR → free 3D-model estimate landing page, unique offer code ("JOON10").
- **EDDM:** ~$0.25/piece postage, **$0.46–0.90 all-in** at small volume [EST]; no permit fee for EDDM Retail. Target by carrier route on eddm.usps.com (older single-family SFV/West LA routes). 1,000-piece test = $500–900. **Only after jobsite-radius canvassing proves out**, targeted to routes adjacent to completed jobs — not blanket ZIP.
- **Truck wrap:** partial $2,500–5,000, lasts 5–7 yrs (~$1–3/day amortized). Near-zero marginal cost since the truck exists — **order the wrap in week 1; do not wait for it before launching LSA/Search** (it's rolling brand, not a gating dependency).
