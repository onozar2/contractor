## Confirmed failures, grouped by cause

**1. No inline `[n]` citations on "lead"/coaching-voiced questions — the dominant pattern.**
foundation-lead, sewer-lateral, hvac-changeout, and design-style-recommend all failed with literally zero `[n]` markers in the answer body on BOTH runs, despite the API returning 6 plausible, on-topic sources every single time. This is not a retrieval failure — the sources are there and are relevant — it's that the model drops citation markers entirely when writing in the "tell me everything before going in" advisory voice. The reference-style questions (beam-replacement, rewire-cost, bathroom-remodel, roof-tearoff, mold-remediation, design-style-spanish) never had this problem — they cited reliably every time. Five more lead-voiced questions (kitchen-lead, repipe-lead, room-addition, waterheater-tankless, design-to-scope) had this exact failure on their FIRST run and only passed because the retry happened to include citations — i.e. this is really a coin-flip on almost every lead-voiced question, not an isolated bug on 4 questions. This is the single highest-leverage fix for the next tune round.

**2. Missing the mandatory "Permits & inspections:" / "Hazmat:" labeled lines — present as prose, not as scannable headers.**
Not a standalone confirmed failure on its own (every case of it flipped to a pass on retry), but it showed up as the flaky factor in kitchen-lead, sewer-lateral (partially), windows-lead, and room-addition. The content (authority name, inspection sequence, hazmat gate) is usually IN the answer, just folded into a differently-named section ("Access & permit basics," "THE PERMIT & COMPLIANCE GATE") instead of under the exact literal line the style anchor calls for. Given how often it flips, this reads as prompt-instruction adherence that's inconsistently followed rather than missing knowledge.

**3. Retrieval misroutes for generic/non-trade-specific questions.**
walkthrough-checklist ("What should I do at every first walkthrough with a new lead?") pulled almost the same 6 sources on both runs — "Lot Due Diligence Before Quoting a New Build," Detached ADU New Build, Soils Report, Fire Sprinklers, Fence material sourcing — none of which are about a generic lead walkthrough. It never surfaced the price-talk-coaching or professional-close content that clearly exists elsewhere in the knowledge base (it shows up successfully in kitchen-lead and phone-ballpark, both of which cite the Bloom sales playbook for that exact material). hvac-changeout has a milder version of the same issue: neither run ever retrieved a dedicated HVAC playbook/product note, pulling Repipe/Rewire/Walls & Fences instead — worth checking whether an HVAC-specific chunk exists in the knowledge base at all.

**4. Narrow content gaps on specific checklist dimensions.**
panel-upgrade never gives on-site walkthrough specifics (panel location, service-drop type, available wall space) in either run — it jumps straight to load-calc and utility-coordination advice. windows-lead never breaks out cost by material grade (vinyl vs. higher-end wood/fiberglass frames) in either run — it only prices by install method (retrofit vs. new-construction). phone-ballpark's qualifying script never asks about timeline or budget comfort in either run, only scope/size/finish/age. design-style-recommend never mentions the app's own Design/AI-render tool as a way to show the client options, even though a source titled "Presenting AI design renders to clients" exists and surfaces correctly for the adjacent design-to-scope question.

**5. Timing.** hvac-changeout (76.5s) and waterheater-tankless (93s) both blew the 75s cap on their first run; both came back well under 75s on retry (28s and 50s respectively), so response time itself is highly variable rather than a systemic slowness problem — but it's worth watching since both were long, citation-and-code-heavy answers.

For the six reference-style questions (beam, pavers-vs-concrete, rewire, bathroom, roof-tearoff, mold) and the two design questions that passed clean, the returned sources were consistently on-topic — roofing questions pulled roofing playbooks and client decks, electrical questions pulled electrical playbooks, etc. Minor noise was tolerable and never cited inline (e.g., a "Trim Paint" scope-of-work source showing up in the mold-remediation source list without being referenced in the text).
