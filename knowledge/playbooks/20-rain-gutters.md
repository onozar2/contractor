# Playbook: Rain Gutters

*When you need this:* existing gutters are sagging, leaking at seams, pulling away from the fascia, or missing entirely, and water is discharging against the foundation or siding instead of being routed away.

## Sequence

1. No permit is typically required for standard gutter installation/replacement. Check with the local jurisdiction if the job changes downspout discharge location near a property line — some cities regulate stormwater discharge onto neighboring property or directly into the street.
2. Remove old gutters, hangers, and downspouts.
3. Inspect the fascia board the entire run — gutters that have been failing often mask fascia rot behind/behind them. Repair or replace any rotted fascia before hanging new gutters; new gutters on rotted fascia will fail again within a season or two.
4. Install new hangers at proper spacing per gutter material/size spec (typically 24"-36" on center, tighter in snow/heavy-rain-load areas — not usually a factor in LA but wind-driven rain loading still matters).
5. Install gutter sections with correct, consistent slope toward the downspout — this is the step that gets rushed and is the single biggest cause of standing water and overflow; there should be no low spots or reverse pitch anywhere in the run.
6. Install end caps, inside/outside miters, and downspout drops, sealing all joints as you go.
7. Determine downspout discharge: tie to a splash block, a drainage extension routed away from the foundation, or connect into an existing French drain/dry well system if the property has one.
8. Seal all seams (end caps, miters, drop outlets) with gutter-rated sealant.
9. Test the full run with a hose, running water from the far end and confirming it flows cleanly to the downspout with no pooling, then confirm discharge point moves water away from the foundation.

## Commonly missed

- Hanging new gutters on rotted fascia without repairing it first.
- Inconsistent slope leading to standing water, overflow at the low point, and premature seam failure.
- Downspout discharge dumping straight against the foundation or onto a neighbor's property instead of routing away — a common source of neighbor complaints and foundation moisture issues in LA's older housing stock.
- Skipping the hose test before calling the job done, missing a low spot that only shows up under real flow.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$8–$16 per linear foot installed for standard aluminum K-style gutters (materials + labor); higher for seamless copper or box-gutter systems.
