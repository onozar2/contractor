# Playbook: Foundation Retrofit (Bolting & Cripple Wall Bracing)

*When you need this:* An older SoCal house sits on an unbolted or under-braced cripple wall foundation and the owner wants seismic protection, often to qualify for insurance credits or an Earthquake Brace + Bolt (EBB) grant.

## Sequence

1. **Assessment / plan.** Determine scope: simple sill-plate bolting on a standard single-story wood-frame house can often follow a prescriptive/standard plan set accepted by many CA jurisdictions without a custom engineer stamp. Non-standard conditions (tall cripple walls, hillside lot, prior additions, irregular framing) require a licensed engineer to evaluate and stamp a repair plan. If the homeowner is applying for the state's Earthquake Brace + Bolt (EBB) grant program, confirm program eligibility and required documentation before starting.
2. **Permit.** Pull permit from the local building authority — LADBS for City of LA, LA County Public Works/Building & Safety for unincorporated areas, or the independent city's building department (Santa Monica, Pasadena, Burbank, etc.). Standard prescriptive retrofits are frequently OTC; anything using the engineered plan path is plan-check. Verify current OTC eligibility with LADBS.
3. Access the crawlspace and foundation perimeter; clear debris, verify working clearance.
4. Test for asbestos/lead before disturbing any suspect insulation, vapor barrier, or painted framing — pre-1990/pre-1978 raise the likelihood of a positive result, but the AQMD Rule 1403 survey requirement applies regardless of building age.
5. Install anchor bolts and foundation plates connecting the mudsill to the foundation at the spacing called out in the plan.
6. Install plywood shear bracing on cripple walls per plan (panel size, nailing schedule, edge distance).
7. Install framing connector hardware — post-to-beam, foundation-to-mudsill straps, hold-downs — at the locations specified.
8. Call for inspection of all installed hardware and bracing before closing up crawlspace access. Do not skip — inspectors need to see the connections, not the closed wall.
9. Close up access, restore any disturbed vents/insulation.
10. Final inspection and sign-off.
11. Compile homeowner documentation (photos, permit final, invoice) for insurance credit or EBB grant closeout.

## Commonly missed

- Treating cripple wall bracing as prescriptive when the wall height or configuration actually requires engineering.
- Closing up the crawlspace before the hardware inspection.
- Missing EBB program paperwork deadlines — documentation has to be lined up before work starts, not after.
- Skipping asbestos/lead testing on pre-1990 crawlspace materials.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$4,500-$9,000 lump sum for a standard single-story prescriptive retrofit (small house, easy crawlspace access); $12,000-$25,000+ for engineered cripple wall bracing on larger or irregular homes.
