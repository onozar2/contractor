# Playbook: Insulation (Attic, Wall & Under-House)

*When you need this:* homeowner has high energy bills, uneven room temperatures, or is opening walls/attic for another remodel and wants to add insulation while access is available.

## Sequence

1. No permit is typically required for insulation alone; if paired with other permitted work (rewire, repipe, remodel), it rides along under that permit — verify with LADBS if unsure.
2. Test existing insulation for asbestos before disturbing it if it's suspect material — vermiculite attic insulation is a known asbestos red flag, more common in pre-1990 homes but not exclusive to them; AQMD Rule 1403's survey requirement applies regardless of building age.
3. Air-seal penetrations first: recessed lights, top plates, duct chases, plumbing/electrical penetrations. Insulating over air leaks wastes most of the investment, so this step comes before any insulation goes in.
4. Install attic insulation (blown-in or batt) to the current Title 24 R-value target for the climate zone — verify the exact number with LADBS/Title 24 documentation rather than assuming a figure.
5. Install wall insulation where accessible: retrofit blown-in through small drilled access holes for existing closed walls (patched after), or standard batt insulation if walls are already open as part of a remodel.
6. Install under-house/crawlspace insulation with an appropriate vapor barrier, accounting for SoCal's below-slab moisture conditions.
7. Verify baffles and ventilation clearance are maintained at the eaves after attic insulation goes in — blocking soffit vents causes moisture and heat problems.
8. Patch any drilled or cut access holes.
9. Clean up and haul debris.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping air-sealing before insulating, which cuts the real-world performance gain significantly.
- Not testing old vermiculite or suspect attic insulation for asbestos before disturbing it.
- Blocking soffit/eave ventilation baffles when adding attic insulation, causing moisture issues later.
- Assuming a specific R-value target without checking current Title 24 requirements for the climate zone.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$1.50–$4.00/sf for attic blown-in or batt insulation; wall retrofit blown-in runs $2.50–$5.00/sf; under-house crawlspace insulation with vapor barrier runs $2.00–$4.50/sf, depending on access.
