# Playbook: Fences (Vinyl / Iron / Wood)

*When you need this:* homeowner wants a new perimeter or side-yard fence for privacy, security, or to replace a failing one — vinyl, wrought iron, or wood.

## Sequence

1. **Verify property line:** locate existing survey pins or prior fence line; this is the most common field problem on fence jobs — never assume the old fence was on the actual line.
2. **Permit:** required once fence height exceeds the local zoning height limit, which differs for front yard vs. side/rear yard and varies by city. Fence height is generally a zoning matter, not just a building-code one — verify the exact limit with LADBS Planning/Zoning (or the local independent city's planning department) before quoting height.
3. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert before setting any posts — post holes are a common spot for hitting shallow irrigation, low-voltage, or gas lines.
4. Layout and mark all post locations per the approved plan/property line, checking string lines for straightness.
5. Dig post holes to the code-required embedment depth (frost depth isn't a factor in SoCal, but embedment ratio to fence height still applies).
6. Set posts in concrete footings, plumb and level, brace and let cure before loading with rails or panels.
7. Install rails/infill per material:
   - **Wood:** install horizontal rails first, then attach vertical pickets with consistent spacing/gaps.
   - **Vinyl:** snap or screw pre-fabricated panel sections between posts per manufacturer spec.
   - **Iron:** weld or bolt panel sections to posts, touch up any weld points before finish coat.
8. Install gate(s) with proper hardware; if the fence encloses a pool area, gates must be self-closing and self-latching per pool safety code.
9. Finish: stain or paint wood; iron typically needs a primer coat plus paint or powder-coat touch-up on any field welds.

## Commonly missed

- Building on the assumed property line instead of a verified one — leads to neighbor disputes and possible fence relocation.
- Not checking zoning height limits separately from building-code fence rules, especially for front-yard fences which are usually capped lower than rear/side yards.
- Skipping self-closing/self-latching gate hardware on pool-adjacent fencing.
- Under-embedding posts, causing leaning fence lines within a few years, especially on taller iron or solid wood sections exposed to wind load.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

Wood: $35-$60/LF. Vinyl: $45-$75/LF. Wrought iron: $75-$150/LF, installed, depending on height and gate count.
