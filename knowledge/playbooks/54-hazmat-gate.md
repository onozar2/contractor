# Playbook: Hazmat Gate — Asbestos (AQMD Rule 1403) & Lead (EPA RRP)

*When you need this:* ANY job that disturbs existing building material by demo, cutting, sanding, scraping, or opening walls/ceilings/floors — this is a cross-cutting gate that applies before the trade-specific sequence in every other playbook, not a standalone job type.

## Sequence

1. **Asbestos survey — required regardless of building age.** South Coast AQMD Rule 1403 requires a survey of materials that will be disturbed by demolition or renovation before that work starts, on any building, any age. Building age (pre-1990 is the common shorthand for LA housing stock) predicts the *likelihood* of a positive result — older texture, mastic, roofing felt, duct wrap, and stucco are more likely to contain asbestos — but it does not waive the survey requirement on newer construction. Treat "the house is newer" as lower risk, not zero obligation.
2. **Sampling:** pull a bulk sample per distinct material/batch (a house with additions or multiple remodels over time may have several different eras of material) and send to an accredited lab. Do not scrape, sand, cut, or wet suspect material before results are back.
3. **If positive:** the work becomes a licensed abatement job. AQMD Rule 1403 requires advance written notification to AQMD before starting (commonly ~10 working days lead time — verify current requirement), a licensed abatement contractor, containment, negative air where applicable, and clearance air sampling before the area can be reoccupied or built over.
4. **AQMD demolition notification is a separate, unconditional requirement** — for demolition work meeting AQMD's thresholds, notification is required even when the survey comes back negative for asbestos. Don't skip notification just because the material tested clean.
5. **Cal/OSHA (DOSH) registration:** any asbestos-related work disturbing more than 100 square feet of asbestos-containing material requires a DOSH-registered asbestos contractor. Confirm the abatement sub's registration is current before handoff — an unregistered sub on a >100 sf job is a real liability and enforcement exposure, not a paperwork technicality.
6. **Lead — EPA RRP applies to pre-1978 housing.** Unlike asbestos, the lead rule genuinely is age-gated: the EPA's Renovation, Repair, and Painting (RRP) rule applies to housing built before 1978 (when lead-based residential paint was banned). For qualifying homes, any work that disturbs more than a minimal area of painted surface (interior or exterior) requires an EPA-certified renovator on site, plastic containment of the work area, lead-safe work practices (wet sanding/scraping, HEPA vacuum, no open flame paint removal, no uncontained power sanding), and a verified cleanup check before the area is released.
7. **Combined jobs:** many demo scopes trigger both gates at once (e.g., 1965 house — asbestos survey on texture/mastic AND EPA RRP on painted surfaces). Sequence both tests before any disturbance; don't let the lead test proceed while skipping the asbestos test just because the house is post-1978-adjacent in the crew's mental model.
8. **Who pays / typical testing cost:** asbestos bulk sample lab testing commonly runs $20-$60 per sample plus a testing-company visit fee (rough total often $300-$800 for a typical single-scope job); lead testing (XRF or lab-sent chip samples) is in a similar range. This is normally billed to the job as a line item, not absorbed — build it into every bid on a pre-1990/pre-1978 property, and budget it as a contingency line even on newer properties where suspect material is found mid-demo.

## Commonly missed

- Gating the survey requirement on "pre-1990" as if it were the legal trigger instead of a probability heuristic — the actual trigger is disturbance of suspect material, at any age.
- Skipping AQMD demolition notification because the asbestos test came back negative — notification and testing are separate requirements.
- Using an asbestos sub who isn't DOSH-registered on a job disturbing more than 100 sf.
- Treating a newer (post-1990) building as automatically exempt from asbestos testing — some materials (certain older-stock imported products, older-formula products used past the common cutoff) can still be positive.
- Conflating the lead rule's genuine age gate (pre-1978, tied to the federal lead-paint ban) with the asbestos rule's lack of one — they work differently and shouldn't be explained with the same logic.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

Testing: roughly $300-$800 for a typical single-scope bulk-sample asbestos test plus lab fees; lead testing in a similar range. If positive, abatement itself commonly adds $2,000-$5,000+ for a contained single-room scope (popcorn ceiling, flooring mastic) and scales up sharply with square footage and containment complexity — always say "verify current abatement pricing with the sub" rather than quoting a fixed number for a job you haven't scoped.
