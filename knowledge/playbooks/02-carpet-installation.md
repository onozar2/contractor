# Playbook: Carpet Installation

*When you need this:* homeowner is replacing worn/stained carpet or upgrading flooring in bedrooms, closets, or living areas.

## Sequence

1. No permit required — carpet is a finish material, no structural/electrical/plumbing scope.
2. Measure all rooms and closets, account for pattern repeat and seam placement; order carpet and pad with 5-10% waste factor.
3. Move and protect furniture; if home is pre-1978 and baseboards will be pried, note lead-paint caution on any paint chips disturbed (see finish note below).
4. Remove old carpet, pad, and tack strip; haul away for disposal.
5. Inspect subfloor: check for moisture staining, soft spots, squeaks, or damage (common near old bathroom/kitchen thresholds and exterior doors in SoCal slab homes); repair or replace damaged subfloor sections before proceeding.
6. Sweep/vacuum subfloor clean of debris and old staples.
7. Install new tack strip around the room perimeter, gap sized to carpet thickness, teeth angled toward the wall.
8. Roll out and cut pad, seam and tape pad sections, staple/tack as needed.
9. Roll out carpet, rough-cut to room shape with margin for stretching.
10. Power-stretch carpet wall-to-wall using a knee kicker at edges and a power stretcher across the field to eliminate ripples and prevent premature wear.
11. Seam sections using a seaming iron and hot-melt tape where a single roll width won't cover the room; match pile direction across seams.
12. Trim excess carpet at walls and tuck into the gap along the tack strip with a stair tool.
13. Reinstall or replace base/toe-kick trim and transition strips (thresholds to tile/hardwood, closet tracks).
14. Final vacuum, walk the seams, replace furniture.

## Commonly missed

- Not checking subfloor moisture before install — a stained or soft spot papered over with new carpet turns into a callback and possible mold issue.
- Under-stretching — carpet installed too loose ripples and wears at traffic paths within a year; power-stretch is not optional on anything beyond a small closet.
- Mismatched pile direction at seams, which shows as a visible color/shade line once light hits it.
- Skipping transition strips at doorways, leaving a trip hazard or exposed tack strip edge.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$4-$7/sf installed (carpet + pad + labor, mid-grade material); add $1-$2/sf for premium pad or pattern-match carpet.
