# Playbook: Retaining Wall

*When you need this:* a slope or grade change needs to be held back — common on hillside lots, terraced yards, or where a driveway/patio cut into grade needs a permanent soil-retention structure.

## Sequence

1. **Geotechnical/soils input:** needed for hillside sites or walls above a modest height — LA's hillside communities commonly require soils engineering input given lateral soil load and slope stability concerns.
2. **Engineer plan:** required for walls above a modest height retaining soil — retaining walls carry lateral soil load and are treated more conservatively than a simple fence or block wall. Verify the exact height threshold requiring engineering with LADBS.
3. **Permit:** required; plan-check is likely rather than OTC given the engineered plan requirement.
4. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert before excavating.
5. Excavate for the wall footing and any working space needed behind the wall for drainage installation.
6. Form the footing and place rebar per the stamped plan.
7. **Footing inspection:** mandatory before pouring.
8. Pour the footing, cure per spec.
9. Install the wall per the engineered system specified — CMU block, poured concrete, or an engineered segmental block system.
10. Install the drainage system behind the wall: perforated drain pipe at the base, gravel backfill, filter fabric wrap, and weep holes through the wall face. This is the single biggest point of failure if omitted — retaining walls without proper drainage fail from hydrostatic pressure buildup behind the wall.
11. Backfill behind the wall in compacted lifts, not all at once, to avoid overloading the wall before it's cured/braced.
12. Cap and finish the wall face per spec.
13. **Final inspection.**
14. Restore grading and landscaping disturbed by excavation.

## Commonly missed

- Omitting or under-building the drainage system — the leading cause of retaining wall failure in SoCal clay soils.
- Backfilling in one lift instead of compacted lifts, overloading a wall that hasn't cured.
- Treating a hillside retaining wall as a standard block wall without geotechnical input.
- Skipping the footing inspection before pour on a plan-check job.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$75-$150 per linear foot for a standard engineered wall (CMU or poured concrete, 3-4 ft exposed height with drainage); segmental block systems can run $100-$200/LF depending on height and hillside access difficulty.
