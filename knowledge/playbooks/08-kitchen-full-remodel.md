# Playbook: Kitchen Full Remodel

*When you need this:* homeowner wants a complete kitchen overhaul — new layout, cabinets, appliances, and finishes, not just a cosmetic refresh.

## Sequence

1. Permit required. Plan-check is likely if walls, gas lines, or electrical panel load are changing; a straightforward remodel with fixtures staying in place may qualify for over-the-counter issuance. Confirm with LADBS (or the local independent city's building dept) early — this drives the whole schedule.
2. Test for asbestos (flooring mastic, popcorn ceiling, old wall texture — pre-1990 raises the odds but isn't the legal cutoff, AQMD Rule 1403 requires a survey before regulated demo regardless of age) and lead (pre-1978 painted surfaces) before any demo that disturbs suspect material.
3. Protect adjacent rooms, disconnect and cap gas/water/electrical to existing appliances and fixtures, shut off power to the kitchen circuits at the panel.
4. Demo: remove cabinets, countertops, flooring, and any wall sections per approved plan.
5. Rough plumbing: relocate/add sink, dishwasher, and icemaker lines per new layout.
6. Rough electrical: add dedicated appliance circuits (range, oven, dishwasher, microwave), under-cabinet lighting circuits, GFCI/AFCI protection per current code at all required locations.
7. Rough gas line work if the range/cooktop is relocated — this requires its own gas-line pressure test and inspection, separate from general rough plumbing.
8. Schedule rough plumbing, rough electrical, and rough gas inspections before closing walls.
9. Insulate exterior walls if opened (Title 24 requirement on envelope work); hang and finish drywall.
10. Install cabinets, leveling and securing to studs.
11. Template countertops on-site once cabinets are set; fabricate offsite (stone/quartz lead time 2-4 weeks).
12. Install countertops.
13. Install backsplash tile.
14. Install and hook up appliances: range/cooktop (gas or electric), dishwasher, hood vent (confirm ducted to exterior, not recirculating, if code/homeowner requires), refrigerator, microwave.
15. Final electrical, plumbing, gas, and building inspections.
16. Paint, install remaining trim/hardware, final punch list and clean.

## Commonly missed

- Not verifying panel capacity before adding dedicated appliance circuits — an undersized panel turns into a mid-job electrical upgrade with its own permit.
- Treating gas line relocation as part of general rough plumbing instead of scheduling its own pressure test/inspection.
- Skipping asbestos/lead testing on an older home because the kitchen "was already remodeled once" — prior remodels often layered new material over old rather than removing it.
- Not confirming hood vent ducting terminates outside — recirculating hoods installed where the plan called for ducted venting is a common inspection fail and CalGreen/ventilation compliance issue.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$250-$450/sf of kitchen floor area for a full remodel (mid-to-upper finish, permit + labor + materials); add 15-25% if the scope includes a structural wall removal or panel upgrade.
