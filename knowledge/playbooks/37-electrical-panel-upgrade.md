# Playbook: Electrical Panel Upgrade (200A)

*When you need this:* an old 60A/100A panel can't support added loads (EV charger, heat pump, induction range, ADU), or the panel is a recalled/obsolete brand (Zinsco, Federal Pacific).

## Sequence

1. Load calculation to confirm 200A is warranted and sufficient — factor in actual current loads plus planned additions (EV charger, heat pump HVAC, induction range are all pushing panel demand up on typical LA homes).
2. Permit application: electrical permit, generally OTC at LADBS (unincorporated County via LA County Public Works/Building & Safety; independent cities pull their own). Include the load calc in the application package.
3. Contact the utility — LADWP within LA city, SCE elsewhere — to coordinate the required service disconnect and schedule any utility-side work (meter pull, service conductor upgrade). This step is the long-pole item: utility scheduling can lag weeks and is the single most commonly missed planning step, so start it the day the permit is filed, not after.
4. Utility disconnects service on the scheduled date.
5. Demo old panel.
6. Install new 200A panel with correct grounding and bonding per code.
7. Coordinate new or upgraded service entrance conductors, and a new meter socket if required by the utility's specs.
8. Reconnect and clearly re-label every circuit at the new panel.
9. Schedule and pass rough electrical inspection (if plan-check required) and final electrical inspection.
10. Schedule utility reconnect appointment.
11. Test the full system once reconnected — verify every breaker, AFCI/GFCI device, and major circuit functions correctly.

## Commonly missed

- Not starting utility coordination early — crew and permit are ready but the LADWP/SCE disconnect appointment isn't, stalling the whole job.
- Skipping the load calculation and defaulting to "200A because that's standard" without checking whether service entrance conductors also need upsizing.
- Forgetting the meter socket may need utility-specific hardware, not just any UL-listed socket.
- Not verifying whether the utility requires a separate service upgrade application distinct from the city electrical permit.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$4,000–$8,500 for a straightforward 200A panel upgrade with existing adequate service conductors; add $2,000–$5,000+ if service entrance conductors or the meter socket also need upgrading.
