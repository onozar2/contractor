# Playbook: Roof Re-Decking

*When you need this:* mid-tear-off on a roofing job, the crew exposes rotted, delaminated, or undersized sheathing that has to be replaced before roofing can proceed — this is almost never sold as a standalone job, it's discovered.

## Sequence

1. This work rides under the existing roofing permit in nearly all cases — it doesn't need a separate permit, but confirm with the inspector on larger scope.
2. Tear off roofing material to expose the deck (covered under the roofing job's own sequence).
3. Walk the exposed deck and flag damaged areas: dry rot, delamination, spongy spots, non-conforming panel spacing or thickness, or damage from prior leaks.
4. If the damage looks like it's touched structural framing (rafters, trusses, ridge/hip members) rather than just the sheathing skin, stop and get engineer sign-off before proceeding — replacing framing is a different scope than replacing sheathing and can trigger a plan-check.
5. Photograph and document the damaged area before removal — this is the backup for the change order.
6. Issue a change order to the homeowner immediately once damage is identified; don't proceed on re-decking scope without signed approval, since this is add-on cost outside the original roofing bid.
7. Remove damaged sheathing back to sound framing.
8. Install new plywood or OSB sheathing matching current code thickness and nailing/spacing pattern for the roof's span and load.
9. Schedule and pass the deck/nailing inspection before any underlayment or roofing covers the new deck — this inspection typically applies to the full deck, not just the repaired section, so coordinate timing with the roofer.
10. Proceed with the roofing installation per that job's own sequence (underlayment, flashing, roofing material, final inspection).

## Commonly missed

- Proceeding with re-decking work before the homeowner signs a change order — this is the single biggest source of billing disputes on roofing jobs.
- Treating framing damage (not just sheathing) as a re-decking fix without looping in an engineer.
- Covering new sheathing before the deck/nailing inspection is scheduled and passed, forcing a costly re-expose.
- Mismatching new sheathing thickness/nailing pattern to current code requirements instead of just matching what was there.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$120–$180 per sheet (4x8, ~32 sf) installed, or roughly $4–$6/sf of re-decked area, typically billed as a change order on top of the roofing contract.
