# Playbook: Whole-House Repipe (PEX/Copper)

*When you need this:* galvanized or old copper supply lines are corroding, leaking repeatedly, or delivering low pressure/rust-colored water throughout the house.

## Sequence

1. Permit: plumbing permit, typically OTC at LADBS (unincorporated LA County goes through County Public Works/Building & Safety; independent cities like Santa Monica, Pasadena, Burbank, Beverly Hills pull their own plumbing permit).
2. Shut off main water supply, drain the system, notify occupants of downtime.
3. Map existing routing — supply origin, fixture branches, water heater connection, hose bibs, irrigation tie-ins if shared off the main.
4. If cutting into painted walls or ceilings built pre-1978, test for lead paint before disturbing surfaces. Test old pipe insulation/wrap for asbestos before disturbing it (common on older water heater and pipe runs) — the AQMD Rule 1403 survey requirement has no building-age cutoff, so treat any suspect wrap as testable regardless of the home's age.
5. Cut access points in walls/ceilings/slab as needed — plan routing to minimize demo (attic and crawlspace runs over wall-opening where possible).
6. Run new supply lines from the main to every fixture: PEX is the standard choice for cost and flexibility in remodel work; use copper where local code, HOA, or client preference requires it, or at exposed/exterior runs.
7. Connect to water heater, all fixtures, hose bibs, and any irrigation branch.
8. Pressure-test the entire new system (typically air or water test held per code duration) before anything gets closed up — this is the core rough inspection and the step most likely to catch a bad joint.
9. Schedule and pass rough plumbing inspection with lines still exposed.
10. Close up walls/ceilings, patch drywall.
11. Reconnect all fixtures (angle stops, supply lines, faucets, toilets).
12. Final inspection.
13. Test every fixture for flow, pressure, and leaks under normal use.
14. Texture and paint patched areas.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping lead/asbestos testing on pre-1978/pre-1990 walls before cutting — required and easy to forget under schedule pressure.
- Not pressure-testing long enough or not having the inspector witness it before drywall closes.
- Forgetting CalGreen water-efficiency fixture requirements if fixtures are being swapped as part of the job (verify with LADBS).
- Underestimating drywall patch scope — repipes open far more wall than clients expect.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$4,500–$12,000+ for an average 2-3 bath single-family home (PEX), scaling with sq ft, number of fixtures, wall material (plaster vs. drywall), and access difficulty; add 20-30% for copper.
