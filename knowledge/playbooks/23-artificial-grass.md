# Playbook: Artificial Grass

*When you need this:* homeowner wants to replace a water-hungry lawn with low-maintenance turf, often for drought/water-conservation reasons or a dog run.

## Sequence

1. **Permit:** not typically required for standard yard turf replacement on an existing lawn. If the job includes significant regrading, new drainage lines, or retaining curbs, check with LADBS (or the local building department) — grading work above a certain cut/fill volume can trigger a permit.
2. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert/USA North 811 before any excavation, even for shallow turf beds — sprinkler lines, low-voltage lighting wire, and irrigation valves are common shallow hazards.
3. Cap or remove existing irrigation heads/valves feeding the lawn area (coordinate with homeowner on any zones to keep for planters).
4. Excavate existing sod and soil to design depth (typically 3-4 inches, more if base needs full rebuild).
5. Grade the sub-base to maintain positive drainage — slope away from the house foundation and toward an approved discharge point (never onto a neighbor's property).
6. Install a weed barrier fabric over the graded sub-base.
7. Install and compact Class II road base (typically 2-3 inches) in lifts, using a plate compactor.
8. Add a leveling layer of decomposed granite or fine crushed rock over the base.
9. Compact again and screed level, checking for consistent drainage slope.
10. Roll out turf panels, allow them to relax, and orient grain direction consistently across seams.
11. Seam adjacent panels with seam tape and adhesive or stitch, then secure the full perimeter with landscape staples/nails on a tight grid.
12. Apply infill (silica sand or rubber crumb, per spec) and work it into the pile with a power broom.
13. Brush/groom fibers upright, trim edges cleanly against hardscape and planters, and do a final walk for high spots or loose seams.

## Commonly missed

- Not capping irrigation lines properly, leading to a leak under the turf that surfaces months later.
- Skipping compaction passes on base material — turf will show ruts and low spots within a year.
- Ignoring drainage direction, causing standing water or runoff onto a neighbor's lot.
- Not checking with LADWP/MWD for current turf rebate program eligibility before demo — rebates often require pre-inspection photos.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$10-$18 per square foot installed (materials + labor), varies with base depth, drainage complexity, and turf grade.
