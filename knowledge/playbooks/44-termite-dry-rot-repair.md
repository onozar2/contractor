# Playbook: Termite & Dry-Rot Repair

*When you need this:* a WDO inspection (often triggered by a real estate transaction) or visible sagging/soft framing reveals termite or fungal dry-rot damage to structural wood.

## Sequence

1. WDO (wood-destroying organism) inspection report identifies the extent of damage and the infestation type — drywood termite, subterranean termite, or fungal dry rot. Note this report and any pest treatment are regulated by the Structural Pest Control Board, separate from LADBS building permits — the repair contractor and the pest control company are typically different licensed entities.
2. Coordinate pest treatment or fumigation first, ahead of the structural repair contract — if whole-structure fumigation/tenting is required, that logistics and scheduling is a separate track from the repair work and needs to happen before framing repairs proceed in the treated area.
3. Pull a building permit for the structural repair work (structural repairs to framing require a permit even though the pest treatment itself did not).
4. Remove damaged framing members — sill plates, studs, joists, fascia, rafter tails — sistering where damage is partial or fully replacing where damage is extensive, per the WDO report's scope.
5. Treat and seal the remaining sound wood at the repair interface to prevent recurrence at the joint.
6. Identify and correct the moisture source that enabled dry rot if applicable — poor drainage, roof leak, missing or damaged flashing. Fixing the framing without addressing the root cause means it comes back.
7. Schedule and pass framing inspection before closing up.
8. Patch stucco, siding, or drywall over the repaired area.
9. Paint to match.
10. Final inspection.
11. Obtain an updated pest clearance report if required for a real estate transaction.

## Commonly missed

- Treating the WDO report and the building permit as the same process — they're separate licensing tracks (Structural Pest Control Board vs. LADBS) and both are needed.
- Repairing framing without fixing the moisture source, so rot returns within a few years.
- Not sequencing fumigation/tenting ahead of framing work when whole-structure treatment is required.
- Skipping the updated pest clearance report when the repair is tied to an escrow deadline.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$1,500–$4,000 for localized sill/stud repair; $6,000–$15,000+ for extensive framing replacement (multiple joists/rafter tails plus stucco/siding patch); fumigation/tenting is billed separately, typically $2,000–$5,000+ depending on structure size.
