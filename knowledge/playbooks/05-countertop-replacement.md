# Playbook: Countertop Replacement

*When you need this:* homeowner wants new kitchen or bathroom counters (quartz, granite, butcher block) without a full remodel.

## Sequence

1. No permit for a like-for-like counter swap in the same footprint. If the scope moves a sink, cooktop, or outlet to a new location, that triggers a plumbing/electrical permit — flag this during the walkthrough so it's priced and scheduled correctly, not discovered mid-job.
2. Site visit to template: measure existing counters, note sink/cooktop cutout locations, edge profile choice, and any change in layout.
3. Order fabrication (quartz/granite/solid surface) — factor real lead time, typically 2-4 weeks from template to install for stone/quartz shops in the LA area.
4. Shut off water supply at the angle stops; disconnect sink, faucet, garbage disposal, and any cooktop gas/electrical connections.
5. Remove sink and faucet; demo and haul away old countertops.
6. Inspect and repair cabinet tops/support structure — old cabinets may need shimming or reinforcement to properly support new stone weight, especially for large islands or overhangs requiring corbels/brackets.
7. Template is typically finalized on-site after demo (digital or physical template) so fabricator cuts to actual field conditions, not assumed dimensions.
8. Fabricate offsite: cut slab, cut sink/cooktop openings, polish edges per selected profile.
9. Install new countertop, ensuring proper support at all edges, overhangs, and seams; set with construction adhesive/silicone per fabricator spec.
10. Reconnect plumbing: supply lines, P-trap, disposal, dishwasher air gap if applicable; reconnect cooktop gas/electrical if it was disconnected.
11. Seal seams (epoxy seam fill for stone) and caulk perimeter (backsplash edge, sink edge, wall edge).
12. Test all connections for leaks; run water and disposal to confirm.
13. Final clean, remove protective packaging/film.

## Commonly missed

- Not flagging a sink/appliance relocation as a permit trigger during the initial walkthrough — this changes both scope and schedule if caught late.
- Underestimating fabrication lead time and leaving the kitchen without a functional sink for weeks.
- Skipping cabinet reinforcement check on old cabinet boxes before setting heavy stone, especially large islands.
- Forgetting to caulk the sink-to-counter and backsplash-to-counter joints — leaving them just sealed with adhesive invites water intrusion into cabinets over time.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$65-$120/sf installed for quartz or granite (materials + fabrication + install); $35-$60/sf for laminate or butcher block.
