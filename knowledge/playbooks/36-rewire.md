# Playbook: House Rewire

*When you need this:* knob-and-tube or old aluminum/cloth-insulated wiring, insufficient circuits for modern loads, frequent breaker trips, or as part of a gut remodel.

## Sequence

1. Permit: electrical permit, typically OTC at LADBS for a standard rewire; plan-check if paired with a panel upgrade or major structural work (unincorporated County via LA County Public Works/Building & Safety; independent cities pull their own electrical permit).
2. Shut off power at the panel.
3. Map existing circuits — what feeds what, panel capacity, any circuits worth preserving.
4. Test wall/ceiling material for asbestos and painted surfaces for lead before opening walls to pull wire — pre-1990/pre-1978 raise the odds but the AQMD Rule 1403 asbestos survey requirement applies regardless of building age.
5. Fish/pull new wire through walls, attic, and crawlspace, minimizing demo where possible; plan a drywall cut-and-patch route for sections that can't be fished blind.
6. Install new outlet, switch, and fixture boxes at correct code spacing and height.
7. Terminate circuits at the panel with correctly sized breakers matched to wire gauge and load.
8. Install required AFCI/GFCI protection — current code has expanded this scope significantly for bedrooms, kitchens, bathrooms, laundry, garages, and outdoor circuits; verify the current AFCI/GFCI matrix with LADBS since it has changed across code cycles.
9. Schedule and pass rough electrical inspection with wiring still exposed and boxes uncovered.
10. Close and patch walls.
11. Install final devices — outlets, switches, cover plates, light fixtures.
12. Final electrical inspection.
13. Test every circuit under load and confirm AFCI/GFCI devices trip correctly.

## Commonly missed

- Assuming old AFCI/GFCI scope still applies — code has expanded room-by-room requirements repeatedly; confirm current version with LADBS before rough inspection.
- Not testing pre-1990/pre-1978 wall material before opening it to fish wire.
- Undersizing the drywall patch budget — full rewires typically require far more wall opening than clients anticipate, especially in plaster homes.
- Failing to label the new panel schedule accurately after rewiring — inspectors and future electricians both need this.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$8,000–$20,000+ for a full single-family rewire (1,500-2,500 sf), scaling heavily with wall material (plaster vs. drywall), attic/crawlspace access, and number of circuits/devices.
