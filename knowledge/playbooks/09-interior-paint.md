# Playbook: Interior Paint

*When you need this:* homeowner wants walls, ceilings, or trim repainted — refresh, color change, or pre-listing touch-up.

## Sequence

1. No permit required.
2. If the home is pre-1978 and any sanding, scraping, or heavy prep will disturb existing paint, follow EPA RRP lead-safe work practices: a certified renovator on site, plastic containment of the work area, wet sanding/HEPA-vacuum methods to limit dust, and a verified cleanup check before the area is released — this is a legal requirement, not optional best practice, on qualifying pre-1978 properties.
3. Protect floors, cabinetry, fixtures, and furniture with drop cloths and masking film; remove or mask outlet/switch covers and hardware.
4. Patch holes, dents, and cracks with spackle/joint compound; let cure fully.
5. Sand patched areas and any glossy or uneven existing paint to promote adhesion (use lead-safe wet-sanding method if pre-1978 surface).
6. Caulk gaps at trim, baseboards, crown, and window/door casings for a clean finished line.
7. Spot-prime bare drywall, patched areas, and any stains (water stains, marker, smoke) with a stain-blocking primer so they don't flash through the topcoat.
8. Cut in edges/corners by hand, then roll walls and ceilings — apply two finish coats for full, even coverage and color accuracy, especially over a color change or dark-to-light transition.
9. Paint trim/doors/casings separately if included in scope, typically after wall coats to avoid overspray/drip onto finished walls.
10. Final touch-up pass once dry, checking under different lighting conditions.
11. Remove masking, reinstall covers/hardware, clean up drop cloths and debris.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping lead-safe containment on pre-1978 homes during sanding prep — this is an EPA-enforceable requirement, not a suggestion, and inspectors/buyers' agents increasingly ask about it on older LA housing stock.
- Only applying one finish coat to save time — shows roller marks, uneven sheen, and poor coverage over color changes, leading to a callback.
- Not spot-priming stains before topcoat, letting water/smoke stains bleed through white or light paint.
- Painting trim before walls are fully cut in, causing overspray/drip cleanup on finished trim.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$3-$5/sf of wall area for standard two-coat interior paint (labor + materials, prep included); add $1-$2/sf if lead-safe containment/EPA RRP practices apply.
