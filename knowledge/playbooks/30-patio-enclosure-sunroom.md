# Playbook: Patio Enclosure / Sunroom

*When you need this:* homeowner wants to enclose an existing open patio cover into a usable indoor-outdoor room (sunroom), typically infilling the perimeter between existing posts with walls, windows, or sliding doors.

## Sequence

1. **Engineer/architect plan:** required for the new perimeter wall infill and window/door layout, tying into the existing patio cover structure.
2. **Permit:** required, and plan-check is likely since this converts open space into conditioned/enclosed space — it triggers Title 24 energy compliance for the new enclosed envelope (windows, insulation, and sometimes HVAC extension).
3. **Habitability distinction:** if the homeowner intends to count this as habitable living space, it must meet ceiling height, egress, and light/ventilation requirements — flag this distinction clearly. An enclosed patio used as a bonus/flex room is a different (lighter) scope than a code-compliant habitable room addition; if full habitable status is the goal, that's a room addition project, not a simple enclosure.
4. Frame new perimeter walls between the existing posts per plan.
5. Rough electrical: outlets, lighting, and possibly an HVAC extension if the space will be conditioned.
6. **Rough inspection** of framing and electrical before closing walls.
7. Insulate walls and ceiling per Title 24 requirements.
8. Install windows and/or sliding doors per the approved plan.
9. Drywall the new walls/ceiling.
10. Install HVAC extension or a mini-split system if the space is being conditioned.
11. **Final electrical/building inspection.**
12. Texture and paint.
13. Install flooring to match adjacent interior or as specified in scope.

## Commonly missed

- Not clarifying upfront whether the homeowner wants a code-compliant habitable room (different egress/ceiling-height requirements) versus a simple enclosed bonus space.
- Skipping Title 24 energy documentation for the new enclosed envelope, which delays plan-check.
- Under-sizing HVAC extension for the newly conditioned square footage.
- Missing rough inspection before drywalling over new electrical.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$120-$220 per square foot for a full enclosure with windows, insulation, electrical, and HVAC extension, depending on finish level and whether full habitable-room compliance is targeted.
