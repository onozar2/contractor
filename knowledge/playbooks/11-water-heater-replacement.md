# Playbook: Water Heater Replacement (Tank & Tankless)

*When you need this:* existing tank water heater has failed or is near end-of-life, or homeowner wants to convert to a tankless unit.

## Sequence

1. Plumbing permit required — typically over-the-counter, same-day at LADBS or the local building/safety dept for a straight replacement; a tankless conversion may take slightly longer if gas sizing or venting changes.
2. Shut off water, gas, and (electric units) breaker power before starting.
3. Drain the old tank; disconnect water lines, gas/electrical connection, and flue/vent.
4. Remove and haul away the old unit.
5. Set the new unit: tank in the existing spot if space allows, or wall-mounted bracket for tankless (needs suitable exterior/garage wall with manufacturer clearance).
6. Connect water lines (cold inlet, hot outlet); replace deteriorated shutoff valves while accessible.
7. Connect gas line — for tankless, verify and upsize the line if the new unit's BTU demand exceeds the existing line's rating (common on conversions, often missed in a quick bid).
8. Install and verify seismic strapping on tank units — California law requires two strap points (upper and lower third of tank) secured to framing; inspectors check this first. Larger tanks (commonly >52 gal) may require a third strap under local amendment — verify with the local building department before final.
9. For tankless, confirm wall-mount bracket is secured per spec and unit is level.
10. Connect venting per manufacturer spec — tankless units need dedicated venting distinct from a tank flue, often sealed/power-vent with specific pipe material and clearances; don't reuse the old flue without confirming compatibility.
11. Install T&P relief valve with discharge line routed to the exterior or within a few inches of the floor — never capped or routed where discharge could injure someone.
12. **Install a thermal expansion tank** on the cold-water supply if the system is closed (CPC 608.3) — most LA/Long Beach homes now have a closed system due to a backflow preventer, check valve, or PRV on the service line, which is easy to miss on a straight swap and is a routine inspection fail if skipped.
13. **Install a drain pan with a drain line to the exterior (or an approved termination) wherever the unit is installed in an interior location or anywhere a leak could cause damage** (garage-over-living-space, attic, closet, interior utility room) per CPC 507.5 — standard requirement on rental units and a common inspector callout on replacements.
14. Confirm the replacement (or new) unit is a **SCAQMD Rule 1121 ultra-low-NOx model** if gas-fired — the South Coast air basin (all of LA/Long Beach/Orange County) prohibits installing a gas water heater that isn't ultra-low-NOx certified; order the unit before the job, since a non-compliant unit can't legally be installed and the job stalls waiting on the right model.
15. Connect electrical if required (power-vent tankless and some tank models need a dedicated circuit).
16. Gas leak test all new connections before firing the unit.
17. Schedule and pass plumbing inspection.
18. Fire up, purge air from lines, verify hot water delivery and, on tankless, flow rate/temperature rise.
19. Label shutoff valves and unit with install date.

## Commonly missed

- Not upsizing the gas line on a tank-to-tankless conversion — tankless units have much higher BTU demand and an undersized line starves the unit or fails inspection.
- Reusing the old flue for a tankless unit instead of installing the dedicated venting the manufacturer requires.
- Skipping or improperly securing seismic strapping — a mandatory California requirement and an automatic inspection fail if missing or anchored wrong; check whether a 3rd strap is required on larger tanks.
- Routing the T&P discharge line incorrectly instead of down and out per code.
- **Skipping the thermal expansion tank on a closed system (CPC 608.3)** — the single most common change-order surprise on a routine swap; if the home has a PRV, check valve, or backflow device, the system is closed and an expansion tank is required.
- **Skipping the drain pan (CPC 507.5)** on interior/damage-prone installs — standard on rental-property jobs and frequently required by local rental-inspection programs (e.g., Long Beach) on top of the base code.
- **Ordering a non-ultra-low-NOx gas unit** — SCAQMD Rule 1121 bans standard-NOx units in the South Coast basin; confirm the model before ordering, not at install.
- On rental property, check for a **local rental-inspection-program overlay** (e.g., Long Beach's rental inspection program) — it can require items beyond base code that a straight swap on an owner-occupied home wouldn't trigger.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$2,200-$3,800 installed for a standard 40-50 gal tank replacement (permit + labor + unit); $4,500-$8,500 for a tankless conversion including gas line upsizing and venting reconfiguration. Add $150-$400 for an expansion tank and $100-$250 for a drain pan/drain line if not already present.
