# Playbook: Tile Flooring

*When you need this:* homeowner wants tile installed in kitchens, entries, bathrooms, or other floor areas — dry or wet.

## Sequence

1. No standalone permit for a straightforward tile-over-existing-floor install in a dry area. In wet areas (shower pans, tub surrounds, wet-room floors), waterproofing work typically ties into a bathroom remodel permit — confirm scope with LADBS (or the local building dept) if the job includes new plumbing or a reconfigured shower.
2. Test for asbestos before demoing old flooring/mastic — old vinyl tile and its black mastic are common asbestos-containing materials in LA homes, especially pre-1990; if positive, hand off to licensed abatement per AQMD Rule 1403 (the survey requirement applies before regulated demo regardless of building age — pre-1990 only raises the odds of a positive, it doesn't waive testing on newer buildings).
3. Remove existing flooring/trim; protect adjacent rooms and fixtures.
4. Inspect and prep the substrate: check for level, cracks, deflection; concrete slabs need cleaning and possibly grinding, wood subfloors need adequate stiffness (tile is unforgiving of flex — undersized subfloor causes cracked grout and loose tile).
5. Install a crack-isolation membrane over any slab with existing cracks, and a waterproofing membrane in all wet areas (shower floors/walls, tub surrounds) — this step is non-negotiable in wet zones and is what an inspector checks if the job is under a bathroom permit.
6. Self-level or float the substrate as needed for a flat plane.
7. Dry-lay tile to plan the layout: center lines, cut sizes at edges, pattern (straight-lay, offset, herringbone), and minimize small slivers at walls.
8. Spread thin-set with a notched trowel sized to the tile, set tile with spacers, back-butter large-format tile to ensure full coverage.
9. Cut tile at edges/obstructions with a wet saw; install transition strips/reducers at doorways and material changes.
10. Let thin-set cure per manufacturer spec before grouting (typically 24-48 hours).
11. Grout joints, tool and clean excess before it cures.
12. Seal grout (and stone tile, if used) after full cure.
13. Caulk (not grout) at all changes of plane — floor-to-wall, floor-to-tub, inside corners — to allow for movement.
14. Final clean and haul-away.

## Commonly missed

- Grouting movement joints instead of caulking them — rigid grout at a plane change cracks as the building shifts.
- Skipping crack-isolation membrane over a slab with visible cracks, leading to reflective cracking through new tile within a year.
- Not confirming subfloor deflection limits on wood-framed floors before large-format tile — undersized joists or thin subfloor causes grout/tile failure regardless of installation quality.
- Missing asbestos testing on old mastic under vinyl/linoleum being removed pre-1990.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$12-$20/sf installed for standard ceramic/porcelain on a dry floor; $18-$30/sf for wet-area work requiring waterproofing membrane and large-format or natural stone tile.
