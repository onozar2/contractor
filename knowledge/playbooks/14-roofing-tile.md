# Playbook: Roofing — Tile

*When you need this:* a concrete or clay tile roof has widespread cracked/slipped tile, failed underlayment beneath sound tile, or the homeowner is converting from a lighter roofing material to tile.

## Sequence

1. Pull a roofing permit — OTC/same-day is typical at LADBS for City of LA re-roofs; unincorporated county goes through LA County Public Works/Building & Safety, and independent cities run their own permitting — verify jurisdiction before scheduling.
2. If converting from shingle or another lighter material to tile, get a structural engineer to verify the existing roof framing can carry tile's added dead load before ordering material — this is the step that gets skipped on "upgrade" jobs and causes framing failures later.
3. Test underlayment/felt for asbestos before disturbing it — pre-1990 is a likelihood heuristic, not a legal age cutoff; AQMD Rule 1403 requires the survey before regulated demo regardless of age, and follow AQMD notification requirements even if the result is negative.
4. Confirm Cool Roof Title 24 compliance for the tile product with LADBS/CRRC rating before ordering.
5. Protect landscaping, hardscape, and gutters; stage tear-off logistics — tile is heavy, plan crane/hoist or careful hand-staging.
6. Tear off old roofing; if reusing existing tile, salvage and stack carefully to avoid breakage.
7. Inspect deck sheathing; repair or replace rotted/delaminated/undersized sections.
8. Schedule and pass the deck/nailing inspection before the deck is covered.
9. Install underlayment — tile roofs typically require a 2-ply system or a high-temp-rated synthetic underlayment given the heat buildup under tile.
10. Install battens if the tile profile/pattern calls for them.
11. Install field tile per manufacturer's fastening pattern — nailed, wired, or foam-set depending on wind zone and slope.
12. Set ridge and hip tile with mortar bedding or mechanical fastening per manufacturer spec.
13. Detail flashing at valleys, walls, chimneys, and all penetrations — this is the highest-leak-risk step on tile roofs.
14. Schedule final roofing inspection.
15. Clean up broken tile fragments, mortar debris, and haul away tear-off material.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the structural load check when upgrading from shingle to tile — tile can run 2-3x the dead load of composition shingle.
- Reusing cracked or spalled salvaged tile to save cost, leading to early callback leaks.
- Under-detailing valley and wall flashing — tile's rigid profile makes flashing transitions harder than shingle and is the most common leak point.
- Not confirming fastening pattern meets current wind-zone requirements, especially on hillside/exposed lots.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$900–$1,600 per roofing square (100 sf) installed for concrete tile, $1,400–$2,200+ for clay tile, including tear-off and underlayment.
