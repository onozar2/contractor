# Playbook: Kitchen Refacing & Refinishing

*When you need this:* homeowner wants a kitchen refresh without the cost/disruption of a full cabinet tear-out — boxes are structurally sound, just dated.

## Sequence

1. No permit needed for refacing — no structural, plumbing, or electrical scope. If the job expands mid-project (layout change, moving appliances), stop and reassess whether a permit is now triggered.
2. Assess existing cabinet boxes: confirm they're square, solid, and free of water damage or delamination — refacing only works if the boxes themselves are sound; rotted or warped boxes need replacement, not a veneer skin.
3. Measure all doors, drawer fronts, and exposed box faces/end panels; order new doors, drawer fronts, and matching veneer sheet stock (or laminate) in the selected finish.
4. Remove all existing doors, drawer fronts, and hardware; label for reference during layout.
5. Clean and lightly sand exposed box faces to ensure veneer adhesion.
6. Apply new veneer skin to exposed cabinet box faces and end panels, trimming flush at edges with a router or flush-trim bit.
7. Install new doors and drawer fronts, aligning reveals evenly across the run.
8. Install new hinges (typically soft-close) and drawer slides if being upgraded along with the fronts.
9. Install new hardware (pulls/knobs) — confirm hole spacing matches new door design or drill new holes as needed.
10. Install matching trim/molding at crown, light rail, and any exposed panel edges to blend old boxes with new finish.
11. Touch-up any exposed interior box surfaces visible when doors are open.
12. Final adjustment of door/drawer alignment, final clean of the space.

## Commonly missed

- Refacing over cabinet boxes with hidden water damage under the sink or near the dishwasher — this surfaces as delamination within months.
- Not upgrading drawer slides/hinges to soft-close when the homeowner expects a "like-new" feel — old hardware on new fronts feels inconsistent.
- Skipping end-panel and interior touch-up, leaving visible old finish where doors don't fully cover the box.
- Assuming refacing stays permit-free if scope creeps into moving an appliance or adding an outlet — reconfirm before proceeding.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$150-$300 per linear foot of cabinetry for full refacing (veneer + new doors/drawers + hardware); $8-$15/sf for paint-only cabinet refinishing without new doors.
