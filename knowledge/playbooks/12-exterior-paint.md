# Playbook: Exterior Paint (incl. Cool Coatings)

*When you need this:* the homeowner's exterior paint is fading, chalking, or cracking, or they want to upgrade to a textured elastomeric/cool-coat system like Tex-Cote.

## Sequence

1. Walk the exterior and flag wood rot, stucco cracks, loose trim, and failed caulk joints — these get repaired before any paint goes on, not patched over.
2. No permit required for a standard repaint or recoat on an existing structure.
3. If the home was built before 1978, assume lead-based paint on exterior surfaces until tested. EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, and Painting) lead-safe work practices are mandatory whenever scraping or sanding disturbs painted surfaces on pre-1978 exteriors — this means plastic containment/ground sheeting, wet scraping methods, HEPA vacuum cleanup, and a certified renovator on site. This is the single most commonly skipped step on older LA housing stock.
4. Pressure wash the entire exterior to remove chalking, dirt, mildew, and loose material; let it dry fully before proceeding.
5. Scrape and sand all loose/peeling paint to a sound edge.
6. Repair wood rot (dutchman patches or full board replacement) and patch stucco cracks — hairline cracks can be bridged by an elastomeric system, but structural or wide cracks need proper stucco patching first.
7. Spot-prime all bare wood, metal, and patched areas with the appropriate primer for the substrate.
8. Caulk all trim joints, window/door perimeters, and siding seams with paintable exterior caulk.
9. Apply the field coat: for standard paint, two coats (body/field color) by sprayer or roller; for a cool-coating system like Tex-Cote, apply per manufacturer's textured elastomeric application (typically a base + finish coat), which bridges hairline stucco cracking and adds reflective/cool-wall benefit.
10. Apply trim and accent colors (fascia, eaves, garage door, shutters, front door) after field coat is dry.
11. Final walk: touch up drips, holidays, and overspray; clean overspray off windows, hardscape, and landscaping; remove masking and protection.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping EPA RRP containment/cleanup on pre-1978 homes — this is a real liability exposure, not just a best practice.
- Painting over unrepaired stucco cracks or wood rot instead of fixing the substrate first — the failure just telegraphs back through in a season.
- Not confirming HOA or historic-district color approval before starting (common in parts of LA with design review overlays).
- Underestimating dry time between coats in coastal marine-layer conditions, leading to lap marks and poor adhesion.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$3.50–$6.50/sf of exterior surface for standard 2-coat repaint; $6–$10/sf for elastomeric/cool-coat systems like Tex-Cote, depending on prep/repair scope.
