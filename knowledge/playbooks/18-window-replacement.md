# Playbook: Window Replacement (Retrofit vs. New Construction)

*When you need this:* windows are failing (seal failure/fogging, broken operation, single-pane inefficiency, rot at the frame) and need replacement — retrofit reuses the existing frame/opening, new-construction removes down to the rough opening.

## Sequence

1. Pull a permit — typically OTC/same-day at LADBS for like-for-like retrofit replacement; new-construction jobs that alter the rough opening size may require plan-check review. Verify with LADBS (City of LA) or the applicable local building department for county/independent-city jobs.
2. Confirm the replacement window meets current Title 24 energy requirements (dual-pane, Low-E coating, argon fill per current standard) — verify exact U-factor/SHGC thresholds and CF1R documentation requirements with LADBS before ordering product.
3. Confirm safety glazing (tempered) is specified for any hazardous-location window per code — near doors, low sill heights, wet rooms (bathrooms), and large panes close to walking surfaces.
4. If using the new-construction method on a pre-1978 home and disturbing painted window trim/frame, treat it as a lead-safe job — test or presume lead paint and follow EPA RRP containment/cleanup practices.

**Retrofit method (existing frame stays):**

5. Remove old sash and any operable hardware, leaving the existing frame in place.
6. Clean and prep the opening — remove debris, check for rot at the sill.
7. Install the new retrofit frame over the existing frame per manufacturer instructions.
8. Shim, level, and fasten per manufacturer spec.
9. Insulate the gap between frames with low-expansion foam or backer rod.
10. Apply code-required caulking seal around the full perimeter.
11. Touch up interior trim/paint at the disturbed edge.

**New-construction method (full frame removal):**

5. Remove the window and full frame down to the rough opening.
6. Inspect and repair framing — sill plate rot is common at older window openings.
7. Install the new window per manufacturer's flanged-installation instructions.
8. Install weather-resistive barrier and flashing tape per proper water-management sequencing (head flashing over tape, tape lapped shingle-style) — this is the step that prevents future water intrusion.
9. Re-stucco or re-side to match the surrounding exterior (tar paper, wire lath, scratch/brown/color coat if stucco — see Stucco Repair playbook).
10. Finish interior trim and paint.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping CF1R Title 24 energy documentation on the permit, causing a final-inspection hold.
- Missing tempered/safety glazing in a hazardous location — this is a common inspection fail.
- Poor flashing sequencing on new-construction installs, leading to water intrusion behind the new stucco.
- Not testing for lead paint on pre-1978 trim before cutting into it with the new-construction method.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$900–$1,800 per window installed retrofit; $1,800–$3,200+ per window for new-construction method including re-stucco/re-siding tie-in.
