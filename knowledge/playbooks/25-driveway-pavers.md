# Playbook: Driveway Pavers

*When you need this:* homeowner wants a paver driveway — either a new install or replacing an existing slab/asphalt surface — for curb appeal without a full concrete pour look.

## Sequence

1. **Permit:** typically not required for a paver driveway laid over an existing driveway or a straightforward new install without structural change. Verify with the local building department if excavation depth is significant or if the job changes drainage patterns.
2. **Encroachment permit:** if the paver work extends into the public right-of-way (the street approach), check with the city's public works/street services department — this can require a separate permit even when the driveway itself doesn't.
3. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert before excavating.
4. Excavate to design depth (base + bedding + paver thickness, typically 8-10 inches total for driveway loads).
5. Install and compact aggregate base course in lifts, checking grade and slope for positive drainage.
6. Install geotextile fabric over the subgrade if soil conditions call for separation/stabilization.
7. Add and screed a bedding sand layer to a consistent depth just ahead of paver placement.
8. Lay pavers per the specified pattern, working off the screeded bed without walking on unset areas.
9. Cut border and edge pavers to fit against the garage, walkway, and landscape edges.
10. Compact the full field with a plate compactor (with a protective pad) to seat pavers and lock the bedding sand.
11. Apply joint sand, sweeping into joints and compacting again to settle it.
12. Seal if specified in the scope.
13. Install edge restraints along all open edges to prevent lateral creep under vehicle loads.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping edge restraints — pavers spread and joints open up under repeated car traffic within a year or two.
- Under-compacting base material, causing rutting/settling in wheel-path zones.
- Not checking encroachment-permit requirements when the paver field crosses into the public right-of-way at the street.
- Wrong base depth for vehicle loading (driveway base needs to be substantially thicker than a walkway or patio).

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$14-$24 per square foot installed, depending on paver material (concrete vs. natural stone) and base depth required.
