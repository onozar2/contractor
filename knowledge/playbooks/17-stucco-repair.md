# Playbook: Stucco Repair & Re-Stucco

*When you need this:* stucco has cracked, spalled, or delaminated in isolated areas (patch repair), or the whole exterior needs re-stuccoing due to widespread failure or as part of a larger job like a window swap or structural remodel.

## Sequence

1. No permit required for isolated patch repair. A permit is required if re-stuccoing large wall areas tied to other permitted work (window replacement, structural repair, additions) or a full-house re-stucco — verify with LADBS (City of LA) or the relevant local building department for county/independent-city jobs.
2. Test for asbestos before major demo or re-scratch work — old stucco and exterior finish coats can contain asbestos, pre-1990 being higher-probability but not the legal cutoff (AQMD Rule 1403 requires a survey regardless of building age); disturbing suspect material triggers South Coast AQMD Rule 1403 notification requirements.
3. Identify and mark all cracked, hollow-sounding (delaminated), or damaged stucco areas; tap-test to find hollow spots that look sound from a distance.
4. Cut out damaged stucco back to sound substrate, squaring the cut edges to make a clean patch line.
5. Inspect and repair the substrate: weather-resistive barrier (paper) and wire lath must be intact and properly lapped — replace any torn paper or rusted/damaged lath per code before re-coating.
6. Apply the scratch coat, keyed into the lath, and allow proper cure time.
7. Apply the brown coat over the cured scratch coat, floated flat and true to match surrounding plane.
8. Allow brown coat to cure per manufacturer/industry standard before finish coat (undercuring is the top cause of finish coat cracking and bond failure).
9. Apply the color/finish coat, texture-matched to the surrounding existing stucco (this is a skill step — texture mismatch is the most visible sign of an amateur patch).
10. Install or repair control joints and caulk them properly to allow for expansion/contraction.
11. Paint the patched area if the existing stucco isn't integral color, blending into the surrounding field.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping asbestos testing on pre-1990 stucco before cutting into it during a re-scratch or major patch.
- Rushing cure time between scratch and brown coats, causing the finish coat to crack within a year.
- Texture mismatch on patches — using the wrong trowel/float technique or aggregate size versus the existing wall.
- Missing or improperly caulked control joints, which is where most future cracking originates.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$8–$14/sf for full 3-coat re-stucco; $500–$1,200 per patch repair depending on size and texture-match difficulty.
