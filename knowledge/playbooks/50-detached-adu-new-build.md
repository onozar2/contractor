# Playbook: Detached ADU New Build

*When you need this:* A homeowner wants a freestanding accessory dwelling unit built from the ground up in the backyard for rental income, aging family, or added living space.

## Sequence

1. **Full architect/engineer plan set.** New foundation design, full structural, electrical, plumbing, Title 24 energy compliance, and CalGreen requirements (water efficiency, construction waste diversion, EV-ready wiring for new construction).
2. **Permit.** Plan-check application with the local building authority (LADBS for City of LA, LA County Building & Safety for unincorporated areas, or the relevant independent city). State ADU streamlining laws have shortened review timelines statewide, and many jurisdictions now offer standard/pre-approved ADU plan sets that skip much of custom plan-check — verify the current program with LADBS before pricing the plan-check timeline.
3. **Utility capacity check and coordination.** New electrical service or subpanel, sewer/water lateral capacity for the added fixture units, and confirm whether the city requires separate utility connections for the ADU.
4. **DigAlert** before any excavation.
5. Excavate and pour the new foundation.
6. **Foundation/rebar inspection** before the pour.
7. Frame the structure and roof.
8. Rough plumbing, electrical, and mechanical.
9. **Rough MEP inspections.**
10. Insulate to Title 24 requirement.
11. **Insulation/energy inspection.**
12. Drywall.
13. Interior finishes — flooring, cabinets, countertops, fixtures.
14. Exterior finish (siding/stucco, roofing) and paint.
15. **Final MEP and building inspections.**
16. CalGreen waste-diversion documentation closeout.
17. Final walkthrough.
18. Address assignment and utility meter setup.

## Commonly missed

- Not confirming sewer/water lateral capacity early — a new ADU adds real fixture unit load and an undersized lateral can stall the project late.
- Assuming full custom plan-check is required when the jurisdiction's ADU standard plan program could cut review time significantly.
- Missing CalGreen waste-diversion documentation, which can hold up final closeout.
- Underscoping the electrical service upgrade needed to support a fully separate unit.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$300-$600+/sf all-in depending on finish level and site conditions (utility runs, grading, access) — a typical 500-800 sf detached ADU runs roughly $200,000-$400,000+ in the LA market.
