# Playbook: Popcorn/Acoustic Ceiling Removal

*When you need this:* homeowner wants the textured acoustic ceiling (common in pre-1990 LA homes) scraped smooth for a modern flat or knockdown finish.

## Sequence

1. **Test for asbestos before touching anything.** Any ceiling texture applied before 1990 (many LA tract homes are 1970s-80s) must be assumed asbestos-containing until a lab says otherwise. Pull a bulk sample per room/texture batch and send to an accredited lab; do not scrape, sand, or wet a suspect ceiling before results are back. Note: the AQMD Rule 1403 survey requirement itself has no age cutoff — pre-1990 is a likelihood heuristic, not the legal trigger; a newer ceiling is lower-probability but not exempt from testing before regulated demo.
2. If the sample comes back positive, stop — this is no longer a standard remodel task. It becomes a licensed asbestos abatement job subject to South Coast AQMD Rule 1403 notification requirements (advance written notice to AQMD, licensed abatement contractor, containment, air clearance) — and AQMD demolition notification is required for the job even when the survey comes back negative. Hand off to an abatement sub; do not proceed with in-house crew. Abatement disturbing more than 100 sq ft of asbestos-containing material requires a Cal/OSHA (DOSH) registered asbestos contractor — confirm the sub's registration is current.
3. If negative, confirm with the homeowner whether the jurisdiction requires any notice for cosmetic scrape work — most cities treat this as non-permit cosmetic work when there's no structural, electrical, or plumbing scope, but verify with the local building department if unsure.
4. Protect the room: cover floors, cabinetry, and furniture with poly sheeting and rosin paper; mask outlets, switches, and light fixture boxes; isolate the work area from the rest of the house with poly and tape to contain dust.
5. Shut off power to ceiling fixtures/fans at the panel; remove or drop fixtures, smoke detectors, and vents as needed.
6. Wet-scrape the texture (light water mist knocks down dust and eases removal) using a scraper and drywall knife, working in sections.
7. Skim-coat the ceiling with joint compound to fill scraper marks, gouges, and seams; sand smooth between coats.
8. Retexture (orange peel, knockdown) or leave smooth per homeowner spec — smooth ceilings need a tighter skim tolerance and more sanding passes.
9. Prime the full ceiling (bare compound flashes through paint without it).
10. Paint ceiling finish coat(s), typically flat white ceiling paint.
11. Reinstall light fixtures, fans, and detectors; restore power; final wipe-down and debris haul-away.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping asbestos testing because the ceiling "looks fine" — texture age, not appearance, determines risk.
- Not containing dust between rooms — popcorn dust travels through HVAC returns and under doors fast.
- Forgetting to test each distinct texture batch separately if the house had additions/patches at different times.
- Not checking whether the jurisdiction wants a notice even for non-structural work — Santa Monica and a few independent cities are stricter than LA city on this.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$2-$4/sf for scrape-skim-retexture-paint (asbestos-negative); add $6-$12/sf if it converts to a licensed abatement job.
