# Playbook: Roofing — Flat/Torch-Down

*When you need this:* a low-slope or flat roof section (addition, garage, porch, main roof on a mid-century flat-roof home) has a failed built-up or modified-bitumen membrane and needs replacement.

## Sequence

1. Pull a roofing permit — OTC/same-day is typical at LADBS for City of LA; unincorporated county routes through LA County Public Works/Building & Safety, and independent cities run their own departments — verify before scheduling.
2. Test roofing materials for asbestos before tear-off, per South Coast AQMD Rule 1403 — the survey requirement applies regardless of building age (pre-1990 just raises the odds of a positive), and AQMD demolition notification is required even when no asbestos is found.
3. Confirm Cool Roof compliance for low-slope roofing — Title 24 applies a different CRRC reflectance/emittance threshold to low-slope than steep-slope; verify the current requirement and product rating with LADBS before ordering membrane.
4. Set up fire-safety protocol before any torch work begins if using a torch-applied membrane: fire watch posted during application and for a documented period after work stops each day, fire extinguishers staged at the work area, no torch work near combustible eaves/vents without extra protection. This is the step most commonly under-resourced on flat-roof jobs and the source of real fire loss claims — if fire risk is a concern, price a cold-applied/self-adhered modified bitumen alternative instead.
5. Tear off the old membrane and inspect the deck for rot or delamination; repair as needed.
6. Verify or install tapered insulation to create positive drainage to scuppers/drains — ponding water is the number-one failure mode on flat roofs, so this step is not optional even on a like-for-like replacement.
7. Install the base sheet, fully adhered or mechanically fastened per manufacturer spec.
8. Install the cap sheet — torch-applied modified bitumen, or a cold-applied/self-adhered system as a lower-fire-risk alternative.
9. Detail all penetrations, drains, scuppers, and parapet wall terminations — these are the highest-leak-risk points on a flat roof.
10. Schedule and pass final roofing inspection.
11. Clean up debris and haul away, tracking CalGreen waste diversion for the tear-off material.
12. Maintain fire-watch protocol through end of day per standard torch-roofing practice.

## Commonly missed

- No fire watch or extinguisher staged during/after torch application — this is a code and insurance requirement, not a nicety.
- Replacing the membrane without addressing drainage slope, so ponding recurs within a season.
- Assuming low-slope Cool Roof threshold is the same as steep-slope — it isn't, verify separately.
- Under-flashing parapet walls and penetrations, which is where flat roofs actually leak.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$8–$14/sf installed for torch-down modified bitumen (2-3 ply) or single-ply systems, including tear-off and tapered insulation where needed.
