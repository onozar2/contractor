# Playbook: Sagging Beam or Post Replacement

*When you need this:* A homeowner notices a sagging floor or roofline, a cracked support beam, or a rotted/undersized post carrying real load and needs it replaced without the structure moving during the work.

## Sequence

1. **Structural engineer evaluation.** The engineer inspects the beam/post, identifies the cause (undersized original member, rot, termite, overload from a past remodel), and stamps a repair plan specifying whether it's a sister (reinforce alongside) or full replace, and the exact new member size/species/grade.
2. **Permit.** Pull permit from the local building authority — LADBS for City of LA, LA County Building & Safety for unincorporated areas, or the applicable independent city's building department. This is structural work, so plan-check is typical rather than OTC.
3. **Build temporary shoring.** Construct a temporary shoring wall or shoring posts to carry the load currently resting on the beam/post before touching the original member. Shoring must be sized and placed per the engineer's direction, on solid bearing — never shim shoring onto an unsupported floor.
4. **Remove and replace or sister the beam/post** per the engineer's plan. If replacing a post, verify the point load below transfers properly to an adequate footing — if the original footing was undersized for the corrected load, it needs to be addressed in the same scope, not left as-is.
5. **City inspection** of the new structural member and its connections before anything gets closed up or the shoring comes out.
6. **Remove shoring**, patch drywall/stucco disturbed by the shoring and beam access, paint to match.

## Commonly missed

- Cutting into the old beam before the shoring is actually carrying the load — verify by eye and by engineer sign-off, not assumption.
- Pulling the shoring before the city inspection has happened and passed.
- Replacing the beam/post but not checking whether the footing below can handle the corrected point load.
- Under-sizing temporary shoring bearing (setting a shoring post on a floor that isn't itself adequately supported).

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$3,500-$8,000 for a straightforward interior post/beam swap with easy access; $10,000-$20,000+ for longer spans, garage/crawlspace beams requiring extensive shoring, or cases where the footing also needs to be upsized.
