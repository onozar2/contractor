# Playbook: Foundation Crack Repair & Underpinning

*When you need this:* A homeowner spots cracking in the foundation or slab and needs to know whether it's cosmetic shrinkage or active settlement requiring structural underpinning.

## Sequence

1. **Engineer evaluation.** This is the step homeowners most often try to skip and it's the most important one. A structural engineer reads the crack pattern (diagonal vs. horizontal, width, offset), checks for differential settlement, and if settlement is suspected, brings in soils/geotechnical input to size an underpinning scope. Set up crack monitoring (tell-tales or gauges) over several weeks if the movement is ongoing rather than historical. Do not let anyone patch a crack before this diagnosis is done — a purely cosmetic repair on an actively settling foundation just hides the problem.
2. **Permit.** Structural repair or underpinning requires a permit from the local building authority (LADBS in City of LA, LA County Building & Safety in unincorporated areas, or the relevant independent city) — plan-check typical given the structural scope. Simple cosmetic crack sealing with no structural component generally does not require a permit, but verify with LADBS if there's any doubt.
3. Excavate at the affected foundation sections per the engineer's plan.
4. **If underpinning:** install new footing sections or piers beneath the existing foundation in sequenced sections exactly as the engineer specifies — never excavate and pour the whole run at once. Sequencing prevents the structure from losing support and settling further mid-repair.
5. **If surface/cosmetic cracks:** rout out the crack and seal with structural epoxy injection (for load-bearing cracks) or polyurethane injection (for water intrusion cracks), per the crack type identified in step 1.
6. Call for inspection of underpinning excavation, forms, and rebar before backfill or pour.
7. Backfill and compact per plan.
8. Patch any disturbed slab, stucco, or landscaping affected by excavation.
9. Final inspection.
10. Monitor per the engineer's recommended schedule (re-check tell-tales at 6 and 12 months) to confirm the repair held.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the engineer diagnosis and going straight to a cosmetic patch on a settlement crack.
- Underpinning an entire foundation run in one continuous excavation instead of sequenced sections.
- Not pulling a permit because the homeowner assumed "it's just a crack repair."
- Failing to monitor after the repair to confirm settlement has actually stopped.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

Cosmetic crack sealing: $500-$1,500 per crack. Engineered underpinning: $1,500-$3,000 per pier/section, with typical residential scopes (4-10 piers) running $8,000-$30,000+ depending on access and depth.
