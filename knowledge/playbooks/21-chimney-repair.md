# Playbook: Chimney Repair / Seismic Strap / Removal

*When you need this:* a masonry chimney has cracked mortar, spalling brick, or no seismic bracing (a well-documented earthquake hazard in LA), or the homeowner wants it partially or fully removed ("chop and cap") instead of repaired.

## Sequence

1. Pull a structural permit — LADBS is typically OTC for a straightforward seismic strap/brace retrofit; removal down to roofline may need plan-check if it alters roof framing or structure. Verify with LADBS (City of LA) or the applicable local building department for county/independent-city jobs.
2. If the firebox, flue liner, or surrounding materials are old and suspect, test for asbestos before demo.

**Repair / seismic strap path:**

3. Assess masonry and flue condition — mortar joint cracking, brick spalling, and flue liner integrity (cracked liners are a fire-safety issue, not just cosmetic).
4. Repair mortar joints via tuckpointing where damage is limited, or rebuild damaged sections of the chimney where cracking/spalling is extensive.
5. Install seismic anchor straps connecting the chimney to roof and wall framing per current bracing standard — masonry chimneys are a well-documented soft-point failure in LA earthquakes, which is why this retrofit is a common standalone request even without other visible damage; verify exact strap spec/attachment requirement with LADBS.
6. Schedule final inspection to sign off the strap installation and any masonry repair.

**Removal path:**

3. Disconnect any active appliance venting (fireplace insert, furnace flue) tied to the chimney before demo begins.
4. Remove the chimney down to the roofline, or below the roofline if a full interior removal is planned.
5. Frame and sheathe the new roof section to match the surrounding existing framing and deck.
6. Flash and tie the new roof section into the surrounding roofing material to match.
7. Patch the interior ceiling and/or wall surfaces where the chimney previously passed through.
8. Schedule final inspection.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the seismic strap retrofit as a standalone job — many older LA chimneys have never been braced and this is a real, well-known hazard, not upsell.
- Not disconnecting active appliance venting before removal, leaving a fireplace or furnace with nowhere to vent.
- Poor roof tie-in after removal, leaving a mismatched patch that leaks at the seam.
- Assuming removal never needs plan-check — if it touches structural roof framing, it can.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$2,500–$6,000 for masonry repair/tuckpointing plus seismic strap retrofit; $6,000–$15,000+ for full chimney removal with roof/ceiling patch, depending on chimney size and interior finish scope.
