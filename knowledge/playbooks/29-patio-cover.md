# Playbook: Patio Cover

*When you need this:* homeowner wants shade and weather protection over an existing patio/backyard area, attached to the house.

## Sequence

1. **Engineer plan:** required if the cover uses a non-standard span, material, or configuration outside typical prescriptive patio-cover tables.
2. **Permit:** required. OTC is typical for a standard attached patio cover of ordinary residential size; plan-check applies for larger spans or engineered designs.
3. Layout and mark footing locations per plan.
4. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert before digging footings.
5. Dig and pour footings for support posts.
6. **Footing inspection** if required by the permit, before setting posts.
7. Set posts, plumb and brace.
8. Install the ledger board attachment to the house framing — this is the most scrutinized inspection point on this job. Flash it properly to prevent water intrusion behind the house's siding or stucco; improper ledger flashing/attachment is a common correction item that will fail inspection.
9. Frame rafters/beams per plan, tying into posts and ledger.
10. Install roofing material per spec — lattice, solid patio-cover panel, or roofing matched to the house.
11. Rough in any electrical (fans, lighting) if included in scope — this needs its own electrical permit/inspection if it's a new circuit run.
12. **Final inspection.**
13. Paint or stain trim/structure per spec.

## Commonly missed

- Improper ledger board flashing/attachment — the top correction item cited by inspectors on patio covers.
- Skipping the separate electrical permit when adding fans/lighting on a new circuit.
- Not verifying post footing depth/size against the engineered or prescriptive table for the actual roof load and span.
- Overlooking setback requirements from the property line for the cover structure.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$35-$65 per square foot for a standard attached wood or aluminum patio cover, installed; solid insulated panel covers run toward the higher end.
