# Playbook: Garage-to-ADU Conversion

*When you need this:* A homeowner wants to convert an existing attached or detached garage into a livable accessory dwelling unit for rental income or family use.

## Sequence

1. **Architect/engineer plan set.** Full plans covering structural adequacy of the existing garage framing and foundation for habitable use, electrical, plumbing layout, and Title 24 energy compliance for the newly conditioned space.
2. **Permit.** Plan-check application with the local building authority (LADBS for City of LA, LA County Building & Safety for unincorporated areas, or the applicable independent city). California's ADU streamlining laws have pushed many jurisdictions toward a faster "ADU standard plan" review track specifically for garage conversions — verify the current expedited/standard plan program with LADBS before assuming full custom plan-check timelines. State ADU law has also reduced parking-replacement and setback barriers that used to block these conversions.
3. **Utility capacity check.** Confirm the existing electrical panel has capacity for the new unit's load, and check that the sewer/water lateral can handle the added fixture unit count from the new kitchen/bath.
4. Test for asbestos/lead before disturbing existing garage materials (drywall, old paint, flooring mastic) — pre-1990/pre-1978 raise the odds of a positive but the AQMD Rule 1403 asbestos survey requirement applies regardless of the structure's age.
5. Demo the garage door opening and frame the infill wall.
6. Add or verify wall/ceiling insulation to Title 24 requirement.
7. Rough plumbing for the new kitchen and bath fixtures.
8. Rough electrical — new dedicated circuits or subpanel (likely required), smoke/CO detectors, possibly a separate meter if required by the city.
9. Rough MEP inspections.
10. Insulation inspection (Title 24 energy compliance).
11. Drywall, then flooring, cabinets, and countertops.
12. Set fixtures (sink, toilet, tub/shower, appliances).
13. Install HVAC — mini-split systems are the common choice for ADUs given no existing ductwork.
14. Final MEP and building inspections.
15. Paint and final walkthrough.
16. Separate address and utility setup as required by the city.

## Commonly missed

- Assuming standard plan-check timelines instead of checking whether the jurisdiction has adopted a standard-plan/expedited ADU track for garage conversions.
- Not checking sewer lateral capacity before committing to a full kitchen + bath layout.
- Underestimating electrical panel capacity, forcing a late-stage panel upgrade change order.
- Skipping asbestos/lead testing because "it's just a garage."

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$180-$350/sf all-in for a standard-finish garage conversion (roughly $90,000-$180,000 for a 500 sf garage), varying with how much of the existing structure/foundation can be reused vs. needs reinforcement.
