# Playbook: Pool Re-Plaster & Equipment

*When you need this:* pool plaster is worn, cracked, staining, or delaminating and needs to be redone, often paired with replacing aging pump/filter/heater equipment.

## Sequence

1. **Permit:** typically not required for re-plaster alone. A permit is required if replacing or upgrading equipment involving new electrical circuits (pump, heater) or gas line work for a heater.
2. Drain the pool per local water-discharge rules — many SoCal cities regulate where pool water can be discharged (storm drain vs. sanitary sewer vs. street); verify with the local public works department rather than dumping to the street by default.
3. Acid wash and inspect the shell for cracks, hollow spots, or delamination.
4. Chip out old plaster down to sound substrate.
5. Repair any structural cracks found in the shell (bring in engineer input if cracking appears significant or structural, not cosmetic).
6. Install/verify VGB Act-compliant anti-entrapment drain covers — required to be checked/updated on any pool work that touches the main drain.
7. Apply bond coat to the prepped shell.
8. Apply new plaster or pebble finish per spec.
9. Replace or service equipment as scoped — pump, filter, heater — running a new dedicated electrical circuit or gas line if upgrading capacity or adding a heater.
10. **Electrical/gas inspection** for any new circuit or gas line work.
11. Acid-start and balance water chemistry on refill per the finish manufacturer's startup protocol.
12. Final equipment test and safety check, confirming drain covers, GFCI protection, and bonding are all in place.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping or forgetting to verify VGB-compliant drain covers during main-drain-adjacent work — this is a code and safety requirement, not optional.
- Draining pool water to the street instead of checking local discharge rules first.
- Not pulling the electrical/gas permit when equipment upgrades add new circuit or gas capacity.
- Rushing the acid-start/water-balance step, causing new plaster to stain or scale prematurely.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

Re-plaster: $6,000-$12,000 for a standard residential pool (white plaster low end, pebble finish high end), plus $2,500-$6,000 if pump/filter/heater replacement is bundled in.
