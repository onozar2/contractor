# Playbook: Concrete Driveway & Steps

*When you need this:* existing driveway is cracked/settled beyond patching, or the homeowner wants new concrete steps to the entry, and full replacement is the right call.

## Sequence

1. **Permit:** required. Standard driveway replacement is typically over-the-counter (OTC) at LADBS (or the local building dept for independent cities); plan-check is likely if you're substantially altering grade/drainage or working adjacent to a structure's footing.
2. **Encroachment permit:** if the driveway approach touches the public right-of-way (where the driveway meets the street/sidewalk), that portion often needs a separate encroachment permit from the city's public works/street services department — this is not covered by the LADBS building permit and is the single most commonly missed permit on this job.
3. **Utility locate:** call DigAlert before any saw-cutting or excavation near the street approach.
4. Saw-cut and demo the existing concrete, haul off debris.
5. Excavate and grade the subgrade to design depth, checking slope away from the garage/house.
6. Install and compact aggregate base course.
7. Form the perimeter and any control-joint layout; form steps separately with riser/tread dimensions per plan.
8. Install rebar or wire mesh reinforcement, chaired up off the base.
9. **Inspection:** footing/rebar (pre-pour) inspection if required by the permit before pouring.
10. Pour concrete, working steps and driveway in a coordinated sequence to keep cold joints clean.
11. Finish per spec (broom finish standard for driveways; stamped or exposed-aggregate if specified) and finish steps with a slip-resistant texture.
12. Cut/tool control joints at the correct spacing to control cracking.
13. Cure (cover/wet-cure per spec), strip forms once cured.
14. Seal if specified.
15. **Final inspection** if permitted work is being closed out.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the separate encroachment permit for the street-approach section of the driveway.
- Insufficient control-joint spacing, leading to random cracking within a year.
- Not verifying step rise/run and handrail requirements against current code before pouring — reworking steps after the pour is expensive.
- Pouring before rebar/footing inspection when the permit requires it, triggering a red tag.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

Driveway: $12-$20 per square foot (broom finish); stamped/decorative $18-$28/sf. Steps: $250-$450 per linear foot of step run, depending on width and finish.
