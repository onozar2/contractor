# Playbook: Wood & Laminate Flooring

*When you need this:* homeowner is upgrading from carpet/tile/old flooring to engineered hardwood, solid wood, or laminate.

## Sequence

1. No permit typically required for a standalone flooring swap. If it's part of a larger permitted remodel (addition, wall move), the flooring is covered under that job's permit — don't pull a separate one.
2. Remove existing flooring, base trim, and any transition strips; haul away debris.
3. Test subfloor moisture — critical on SoCal slab-on-grade homes, where a concrete slab with no vapor barrier can wick moisture into new wood flooring for years after the pour. Use a calcium chloride test or moisture meter and confirm readings are within the flooring manufacturer's spec before proceeding; this determines whether a vapor barrier/membrane is mandatory.
4. Level the subfloor — grind high spots, use self-leveling compound on low spots (concrete) or patch/replace damaged sheathing (wood subfloor).
5. Install vapor barrier/underlayment per product spec — required on slab installs, and standard practice over wood subfloor for sound/moisture control.
6. Acclimate flooring material to the room's temperature/humidity per manufacturer instructions (typically 48-72 hours on-site) before install, especially for solid wood.
7. Install flooring per system type: click-lock floating engineered/laminate (no fasteners to subfloor, floats over underlayment), or nail-down/glue-down engineered hardwood (fastened or adhered directly to subfloor).
8. Rack boards for color/grain variation, stagger end joints per manufacturer minimum offset.
9. Scribe and cut around obstructions (cabinets, HVAC registers, door casings); undercut door jambs and casings rather than notching flooring around them.
10. Install expansion gap at all walls and fixed obstructions per product spec (floating floors especially need this to prevent buckling).
11. Install transition strips/reducers at doorways and material changes.
12. Reinstall or replace base/shoe trim to cover the expansion gap.
13. Final clean, remove protective film if present.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the subfloor moisture test on a slab — this is the #1 cause of cupping/buckling callbacks in SoCal installs and can void the flooring warranty.
- Not leaving expansion gaps at walls, especially on floating floors — the floor buckles as it expands seasonally.
- Failing to acclimate solid wood long enough, especially when delivered during Santa Ana dry spells or right after a rainy stretch.
- Nailing/gluing directly over a subfloor with unresolved squeaks or damage instead of fixing it first.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$8-$14/sf installed for laminate/engineered click-lock; $12-$20/sf for nail-down/glue-down engineered or solid hardwood, materials + labor.
