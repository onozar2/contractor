# Playbook: Mold Removal & Remediation

*When you need this:* visible mold growth or a musty odor traced to a leak, poor ventilation, or chronic moisture — bathroom, attic, crawlspace, or wall cavity.

## Sequence

1. No standalone building permit for remediation itself. But repairing the underlying moisture source — roof leak, plumbing leak, failed flashing, inadequate ventilation — may require its own permit if structural, roofing, or plumbing work is involved; pull that separately.
2. Identify and document the extent: moisture meter to map affected framing/drywall, photograph growth, and on larger jobs or anything triggering an insurance claim/tenant dispute, bring in a third-party mold assessor for lab-tested sampling and a written protocol before work starts.
3. Fix the water source first. Remediating without stopping the moisture feeding it is a guaranteed repeat job.
4. Set up containment: poly sheeting to isolate the work area; negative air pressure with a HEPA-filtered scrubber for anything beyond a small, contained spot.
5. Remove affected porous materials — drywall, insulation, carpet/pad, wood if compromised — bagging and disposing per protocol; colonized porous material needs to come out, not be cleaned.
6. HEPA-vacuum exposed framing and adjacent surfaces to remove spore residue.
7. Apply antimicrobial/biocide treatment to remaining framing per product label.
8. Dry out the cavity completely — dehumidifiers and air movers until moisture readings confirm framing is normal before closing anything up.
9. Reinstall insulation and drywall once dry-out is verified.
10. Texture-match and paint to blend with surrounding finish.
11. On larger jobs, get a post-remediation clearance test confirming spore counts are normal before closing the job.

## Commonly missed

- Closing up drywall before moisture-meter readings confirm the cavity is fully dry — traps residual moisture and restarts the problem behind new material.
- Remediating the mold without permanently fixing the water source, so it recurs within a season.
- Skipping containment/negative air on jobs that look "small" but involve cutting into moldy drywall, spreading spores through the HVAC system.
- Not documenting extent with photos/moisture readings before demo — matters for insurance claims and for proving scope was appropriate if a homeowner disputes the invoice later.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$10-$25/sf of affected area for remediation (containment + removal + treatment + dry-out), separate from the cost of repairing the underlying leak/ventilation source.
