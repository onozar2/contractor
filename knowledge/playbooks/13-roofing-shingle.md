# Playbook: Roofing — Composition Shingle

*When you need this:* an asphalt/composition shingle roof is past its service life — curling, granule loss, active leaks — and needs full tear-off and replacement.

## Sequence

1. Pull a roofing permit before starting work — for City of LA this is LADBS, typically over-the-counter/same-day for a like-for-like re-roof; unincorporated LA County goes through LA County Public Works/Building & Safety, and independent cities (Santa Monica, Pasadena, Burbank, Beverly Hills, etc.) have their own building departments — verify jurisdiction and OTC eligibility before scheduling.
2. Test the existing roofing/underlayment for asbestos before tear-off (pre-1990 is higher-probability but not the legal trigger — AQMD Rule 1403 requires a survey regardless of building age); disturbing suspect material triggers South Coast AQMD Rule 1403 notification requirements, and demolition notification is required even when the survey comes back negative.
3. Confirm Cool Roof compliance up front — Title 24 requires a CRRC-rated shingle product in most LA climate zones for re-roofs; verify the current reflectance/emittance threshold and product rating with LADBS before ordering material.
4. Set up tear-off protection: tarp landscaping, protect gutters, stage a debris chute or dumpster.
5. Tear off old shingles, underlayment, and flashing down to the deck.
6. Inspect the deck; repair or replace any rotted, delaminated, or undersized sheathing (see Roof Re-Decking playbook if scope is significant).
7. Schedule and pass the deck/nailing inspection — most jurisdictions require the bare deck and nailing pattern be inspected before it's covered.
8. Install underlayment (synthetic felt or self-adhered ice-and-water membrane at eaves/valleys per manufacturer spec).
9. Install drip edge at eaves and rakes, and step/valley flashing at all walls, chimneys, and roof-to-wall transitions.
10. Install shingles per manufacturer's nailing pattern and exposure, starting from the eave.
11. Install ridge vent, pipe boots, and all penetration flashing details.
12. Schedule final roofing inspection.
13. Magnetic nail sweep of the entire property, including driveway and adjacent walkways.
14. Haul and dispose of debris, tracking waste diversion for CalGreen recycling requirements on the tear-off material.

## Commonly missed

- Skipping the asbestos survey before tear-off (the Rule 1403 survey has no age cutoff - older roofs are just higher-probability) — this is a compliance and crew-safety issue, not optional.
- Skipping or forgetting to schedule the deck nailing inspection before covering with underlayment — this forces re-exposing the deck.
- Assuming the old shingle product satisfies current Cool Roof CRRC requirements — always verify the new product's rating meets today's Title 24 threshold.
- No magnetic nail sweep, leading to flat tires and liability claims.

## Rough SoCal cost (2025-26)

$550–$900 per roofing square (100 sf) installed, or roughly $6–$10/sf including tear-off, deck repair allowance, and permit.
