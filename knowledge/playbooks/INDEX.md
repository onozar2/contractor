# SoCal Job Playbooks — Index

One playbook per common Southern California residential home-improvement job. Each covers engineer/plans (if applicable), permit authority, prep/hazmat, trade-order work steps, inspections, finish/cleanup, commonly missed items, and a rough 2025-26 SoCal cost range.

- **Popcorn/Acoustic Ceiling Removal** — [01-popcorn-ceiling-removal.md](./01-popcorn-ceiling-removal.md) — homeowner wants the textured acoustic ceiling (common in pre-1990 LA homes) scraped smooth for a modern flat or knockdown finish.
- **Carpet Installation** — [02-carpet-installation.md](./02-carpet-installation.md) — homeowner is replacing worn/stained carpet or upgrading flooring in bedrooms, closets, or living areas.
- **Wood & Laminate Flooring** — [03-wood-laminate-flooring.md](./03-wood-laminate-flooring.md) — homeowner is upgrading from carpet/tile/old flooring to engineered hardwood, solid wood, or laminate.
- **Tile Flooring** — [04-tile-flooring.md](./04-tile-flooring.md) — homeowner wants tile installed in kitchens, entries, bathrooms, or other floor areas — dry or wet.
- **Countertop Replacement** — [05-countertop-replacement.md](./05-countertop-replacement.md) — homeowner wants new kitchen or bathroom counters (quartz, granite, butcher block) without a full remodel.
- **Kitchen Refacing & Refinishing** — [06-kitchen-refacing-refinishing.md](./06-kitchen-refacing-refinishing.md) — homeowner wants a kitchen refresh without the cost/disruption of a full cabinet tear-out — boxes are structurally sound, just dated.
- **Bathroom Remodel** — [07-bathroom-remodel.md](./07-bathroom-remodel.md) — homeowner wants a full bathroom gut-and-rebuild — new layout, fixtures, tile, and finishes.
- **Kitchen Full Remodel** — [08-kitchen-full-remodel.md](./08-kitchen-full-remodel.md) — homeowner wants a complete kitchen overhaul — new layout, cabinets, appliances, and finishes, not just a cosmetic refresh.
- **Interior Paint** — [09-interior-paint.md](./09-interior-paint.md) — homeowner wants walls, ceilings, or trim repainted — refresh, color change, or pre-listing touch-up.
- **Mold Removal & Remediation** — [10-mold-removal.md](./10-mold-removal.md) — visible mold growth or a musty odor traced to a leak, poor ventilation, or chronic moisture — bathroom, attic, crawlspace, or wall cavity.
- **Water Heater Replacement (Tank & Tankless)** — [11-water-heater-replacement.md](./11-water-heater-replacement.md) — existing tank water heater has failed or is near end-of-life, or homeowner wants to convert to a tankless unit.
- **Exterior Paint (incl. Cool Coatings)** — [12-exterior-paint.md](./12-exterior-paint.md) — the homeowner's exterior paint is fading, chalking, or cracking, or they want to upgrade to a textured elastomeric/cool-coat system like Tex-Cote.
- **Roofing — Composition Shingle** — [13-roofing-shingle.md](./13-roofing-shingle.md) — an asphalt/composition shingle roof is past its service life — curling, granule loss, active leaks — and needs full tear-off and replacement.
- **Roofing — Tile** — [14-roofing-tile.md](./14-roofing-tile.md) — a concrete or clay tile roof has widespread cracked/slipped tile, failed underlayment beneath sound tile, or the homeowner is converting from a lighter roofing material to tile.
- **Roofing — Flat/Torch-Down** — [15-roofing-flat-torch-down.md](./15-roofing-flat-torch-down.md) — a low-slope or flat roof section (addition, garage, porch, main roof on a mid-century flat-roof home) has a failed built-up or modified-bitumen membrane and needs replacement.
- **Roof Re-Decking** — [16-re-decking.md](./16-re-decking.md) — mid-tear-off on a roofing job, the crew exposes rotted, delaminated, or undersized sheathing that has to be replaced before roofing can proceed — this is almost never sold as a standalone job, it's discovered.
- **Stucco Repair & Re-Stucco** — [17-stucco-repair.md](./17-stucco-repair.md) — stucco has cracked, spalled, or delaminated in isolated areas (patch repair), or the whole exterior needs re-stuccoing due to widespread failure or as part of a larger job like a window swap or structural remodel.
- **Window Replacement (Retrofit vs. New Construction)** — [18-window-replacement.md](./18-window-replacement.md) — windows are failing (seal failure/fogging, broken operation, single-pane inefficiency, rot at the frame) and need replacement — retrofit reuses the existing frame/opening, new-construction removes down to the rough opening.
- **Skylight Install** — [19-skylight-install.md](./19-skylight-install.md) — a homeowner wants natural light added to a hallway, bathroom, or kitchen with no exterior wall access, requiring a new roof and ceiling opening.
- **Rain Gutters** — [20-rain-gutters.md](./20-rain-gutters.md) — existing gutters are sagging, leaking at seams, pulling away from the fascia, or missing entirely, and water is discharging against the foundation or siding instead of being routed away.
- **Chimney Repair / Seismic Strap / Removal** — [21-chimney-repair.md](./21-chimney-repair.md) — a masonry chimney has cracked mortar, spalling brick, or no seismic bracing (a well-documented earthquake hazard in LA), or the homeowner wants it partially or fully removed ("chop and cap") instead of repaired.
- **Garage Door Replacement** — [22-garage-door-replacement.md](./22-garage-door-replacement.md) — an old garage door is damaged, off-track, or inefficient (uninsulated single-panel), and needs replacement with a like-for-like or upgraded insulated door.
- **Artificial Grass** — [23-artificial-grass.md](./23-artificial-grass.md) — homeowner wants to replace a water-hungry lawn with low-maintenance turf, often for drought/water-conservation reasons or a dog run.
- **Concrete Driveway & Steps** — [24-concrete-driveway-steps.md](./24-concrete-driveway-steps.md) — existing driveway is cracked/settled beyond patching, or the homeowner wants new concrete steps to the entry, and full replacement is the right call.
- **Driveway Pavers** — [25-driveway-pavers.md](./25-driveway-pavers.md) — homeowner wants a paver driveway — either a new install or replacing an existing slab/asphalt surface — for curb appeal without a full concrete pour look.
- **Fences (Vinyl / Iron / Wood)** — [26-fences.md](./26-fences.md) — homeowner wants a new perimeter or side-yard fence for privacy, security, or to replace a failing one — vinyl, wrought iron, or wood.
- **Block Wall** — [27-block-wall.md](./27-block-wall.md) — homeowner wants a CMU (concrete masonry unit) wall for property-line enclosure, privacy, or a low garden wall that is not retaining significant soil.
- **Retaining Wall** — [28-retaining-wall.md](./28-retaining-wall.md) — a slope or grade change needs to be held back — common on hillside lots, terraced yards, or where a driveway/patio cut into grade needs a permanent soil-retention structure.
- **Patio Cover** — [29-patio-cover.md](./29-patio-cover.md) — homeowner wants shade and weather protection over an existing patio/backyard area, attached to the house.
- **Patio Enclosure / Sunroom** — [30-patio-enclosure-sunroom.md](./30-patio-enclosure-sunroom.md) — homeowner wants to enclose an existing open patio cover into a usable indoor-outdoor room (sunroom), typically infilling the perimeter between existing posts with walls, windows, or sliding doors.
- **French Drain & Site Drainage** — [31-french-drain-site-drainage.md](./31-french-drain-site-drainage.md) — yard holds standing water, water pools against the foundation, or a low spot never drains after rain — needs a designed drainage path instead of relying on grade alone.
- **Pool Re-Plaster & Equipment** — [32-pool-replaster-equipment.md](./32-pool-replaster-equipment.md) — pool plaster is worn, cracked, staining, or delaminating and needs to be redone, often paired with replacing aging pump/filter/heater equipment.
- **Balcony / Deck Waterproofing (incl. SB326/SB721)** — [33-balcony-deck-waterproofing.md](./33-balcony-deck-waterproofing.md) — an elevated balcony, deck, walkway, or stairway with a waterproof walking surface is showing cracked coating, soft/spongy decking, staining on the framing below, or is due for its mandatory inspection under California's exterior elevated element (EEE) laws.
- **Whole-House Repipe (PEX/Copper)** — [34-whole-house-repipe.md](./34-whole-house-repipe.md) — galvanized or old copper supply lines are corroding, leaking repeatedly, or delivering low pressure/rust-colored water throughout the house.
- **Re-Drain (Waste/Drain Line Replacement)** — [35-re-drain.md](./35-re-drain.md) — recurring backups, slow drains, sewer odor, or a camera inspection showing cracked/bellied cast iron or corroded galvanized drain lines.
- **House Rewire** — [36-rewire.md](./36-rewire.md) — knob-and-tube or old aluminum/cloth-insulated wiring, insufficient circuits for modern loads, frequent breaker trips, or as part of a gut remodel.
- **Electrical Panel Upgrade (200A)** — [37-electrical-panel-upgrade.md](./37-electrical-panel-upgrade.md) — an old 60A/100A panel can't support added loads (EV charger, heat pump, induction range, ADU), or the panel is a recalled/obsolete brand (Zinsco, Federal Pacific).
- **EV Charger Circuit** — [38-ev-charger-circuit.md](./38-ev-charger-circuit.md) — homeowner buys an EV and needs a dedicated 240V circuit run to the garage or driveway for a Level 2 charger.
- **HVAC Changeout / Heat-Pump Conversion** — [39-hvac-changeout-heat-pump.md](./39-hvac-changeout-heat-pump.md) — existing furnace/AC or straight AC system is end-of-life, inefficient, or the homeowner wants to convert from gas to an electric heat pump.
- **Air Duct Replacement** — [40-air-duct-replacement.md](./40-air-duct-replacement.md) — old ducts are crushed, disconnected, leaking heavily, or wrapped in deteriorating insulation in an attic/crawlspace, causing poor airflow and high energy bills.
- **Solar PV Installation** — [41-solar-pv.md](./41-solar-pv.md) — homeowner wants to install rooftop solar to offset electric bills, often paired with an EV or heat-pump conversion that's pushing electric load up.
- **Insulation (Attic, Wall & Under-House)** — [42-insulation-attic-wall-underfloor.md](./42-insulation-attic-wall-underfloor.md) — homeowner has high energy bills, uneven room temperatures, or is opening walls/attic for another remodel and wants to add insulation while access is available.
- **Sewer Lateral Replacement (Trench vs. Trenchless)** — [43-sewer-lateral-replacement.md](./43-sewer-lateral-replacement.md) — a camera inspection or recurring backups reveal a collapsed, root-infiltrated, or badly offset sewer lateral between the house and the city main.
- **Termite & Dry-Rot Repair** — [44-termite-dry-rot-repair.md](./44-termite-dry-rot-repair.md) — a WDO inspection (often triggered by a real estate transaction) or visible sagging/soft framing reveals termite or fungal dry-rot damage to structural wood.
- **Foundation Retrofit (Bolting & Cripple Wall Bracing)** — [45-foundation-retrofit.md](./45-foundation-retrofit.md) — An older SoCal house sits on an unbolted or under-braced cripple wall foundation and the owner wants seismic protection, often to qualify for insurance credits or an Earthquake Brace + Bolt (EBB) grant.
- **Foundation Crack Repair & Underpinning** — [46-foundation-crack-repair-underpinning.md](./46-foundation-crack-repair-underpinning.md) — A homeowner spots cracking in the foundation or slab and needs to know whether it's cosmetic shrinkage or active settlement requiring structural underpinning.
- **Sagging Beam or Post Replacement** — [47-sagging-beam-post-replacement.md](./47-sagging-beam-post-replacement.md) — A homeowner notices a sagging floor or roofline, a cracked support beam, or a rotted/undersized post carrying real load and needs it replaced without the structure moving during the work.
- **Sistering Floor Joists & Subfloor Repair** — [48-sistering-floor-joists-subfloor.md](./48-sistering-floor-joists-subfloor.md) — Floor joists or subfloor are damaged by dry rot, termites, or long-term water intrusion — often discovered mid-demo during a bathroom or kitchen remodel, not as a standalone job.
- **Garage-to-ADU Conversion** — [49-garage-to-adu-conversion.md](./49-garage-to-adu-conversion.md) — A homeowner wants to convert an existing attached or detached garage into a livable accessory dwelling unit for rental income or family use.
- **Detached ADU New Build** — [50-detached-adu-new-build.md](./50-detached-adu-new-build.md) — A homeowner wants a freestanding accessory dwelling unit built from the ground up in the backyard for rental income, aging family, or added living space.
- **Room Addition (Ground Floor)** — [51-room-addition.md](./51-room-addition.md) — A homeowner wants to expand the existing footprint with a new ground-floor room — bedroom, family room, expanded kitchen — tied into the existing house.
- **Second-Story Addition** — [52-second-story-addition.md](./52-second-story-addition.md) — A homeowner wants to add a second floor over some or all of an existing single-story house rather than build out — the most structurally demanding job type in the playbook set.
- **Zoning Gate — What to Check Before Quoting** — [53-zoning-basics-la.md](./53-zoning-basics-la.md) — reference to run before quoting any job that adds height, footprint, or a structure near a property line (fences/walls, patio covers, ADUs, additions, second stories) — front/side/rear height limits, setbacks, hillside/HPOZ/coastal overlays, and how to check ZIMAS.
- **Hazmat Gate — Asbestos (AQMD Rule 1403) & Lead (EPA RRP)** — [54-hazmat-gate.md](./54-hazmat-gate.md) — cross-cutting reference for any job disturbing existing material by demo, cutting, sanding, or opening walls/ceilings/floors — the asbestos survey has no building-age cutoff, lead RRP rules are pre-1978, DOSH registration above 100 sf.
- **SB721 vs SB326 Cheat Sheet** — [55-balcony-inspection-law.md](./55-balcony-inspection-law.md) — quick reference alongside the Balcony/Deck Waterproofing playbook: who each statute covers, who may inspect, the January 1, 2026 deadline, re-inspection cycles, and who receives the report.
