# Water Heaters

## The ladder

**Storage tank (gas, standard vent), installed:**
- **Good — 40-gal atmospheric-vent tank, 0.58-0.64 UEF** (Rheem Performance / AO Smith Signature-class): ~$1,600-$2,400 installed. Baseline code-legal replacement for a condo or small household. Must still be an ultra-low-NOx model to sell legally in the LA basin (see Watch Out).
- **Better — 50-gal high-efficiency atmospheric or power-vent tank, 0.64-0.70 UEF** (Rheem Professional / AO Smith Effex-class): ~$2,200-$3,200 installed. The default recommendation for a 3-4 bed household — more first-hour delivery, modestly better UEF.
- **Best — 50-65 gal hybrid heat-pump water heater, 3.0-4.0 UEF** (Rheem ProTerra, AO Smith Voltex, Bradford White AeroTherm): ~$4,500-$9,500 installed in LA, per current 2025-2026 quotes — the wide range is driven by whether it's a straight swap or needs a dedicated 240V circuit, condensate drain, and clearance work. Runs 3-4x more efficient than gas, no combustion/venting at all, so it sidesteps SCAQMD NOx rules entirely.

**Tankless, installed:**
- **Better — non-condensing tankless, 0.80-0.85 UEF** (Rinnai/Navien entry lines): ~$2,400-$3,500 installed. Needs stainless steel venting ($15-30+/linear ft), which often erases the equipment savings vs. condensing.
- **Best — condensing tankless, 0.90-0.96 UEF** (Rinnai Sensei/CU-series, Navien NPE-A2/NPE-S2): ~$3,000-$5,000 installed. Cheap PVC venting, sold as a 15-20 year unit, and this is the tier that unlocks a recirculation loop.
- **Recirculation add-on (instant hot water at fixtures):** +$600-$1,500 installed for a dedicated recirc line/pump, or +$300-$600 for a comfort-valve retrofit using the existing cold line (no dedicated return line needed) — the retrofit is the practical upsell on most single-story LA houses.

## How to choose

- Condo/small household, tight budget, no interest in electrification → 40-gal or 50-gal standard tank.
- 3-4 bed family home, standard panel, no appetite for panel upgrade → 50-gal high-efficiency tank or non-condensing tankless.
- Client mentions "running out of hot water," big soaking tub, or multiple bathrooms used back-to-back → tankless (condensing) sized to simultaneous fixture draw, or a 65-gal hybrid heat pump.
- Electrification-motivated client, all-electric remodel, or panel already has room/being upgraded → hybrid heat-pump water heater — it's also the only tier fully insulated from the coming SCAQMD gas phase-out.
- Primary bath or kitchen is far from the water heater (long pipe runs, common in single-story ranch layouts) → recirculation add-on regardless of unit tier.

## Upsell notes

"A gas tank pushes hot water to one place at a time — showers, dishwasher, and laundry running together and you feel it. Tankless doesn't run out, it just keeps making hot water as long as the fixture's open." For the heat pump: "This one pulls heat out of the garage air instead of burning gas — it's the same technology as your fridge, run backwards. Costs more up front, but it's a fraction of your gas bill and it's not exposed to what the air district's about to do to gas water heaters." For recirc: "Right now you're running the tap and waiting — this gets hot water to that faucet in seconds instead of running thirty seconds down the drain first."

## Watch out

- **SCAQMD Rule 1121 (South Coast Air Basin, covers all of LA):** current residential gas water heater NOx limit is ultra-low — 14 ng/J (about 20 ppmv) for most tankless units up to 400,000 Btu/hr, and 10 ng/J (about 15 ppmv) for smaller storage units at or under 75,000 Btu/hr input. Standard (non-ultra-low-NOx) gas water heaters are already illegal to install new in the district — confirm any quoted gas unit is explicitly labeled ultra-low-NOx before ordering. SCAQMD has proposed amendments (PAR 1121) moving toward zero-NOx (effectively no new gas water heaters) on a phased timeline starting with new construction — verify current spec/effective dates before quoting any job more than a few months out, this is actively moving.
- Tankless condensing units produce acidic condensate (pH 3-5) and need a neutralizer cartridge plus a drain path (floor drain or condensate pump) — commonly required, factor it into the quote.
- Switching gas tank → tankless on an older house often triggers a gas line upsize (3/4" to sometimes larger) since tankless units draw much higher BTU/hr instantaneously — inspect the existing line before quoting a flat price.
- Heat pump water heaters need airflow clearance (typically 700-1,000 cubic ft of surrounding air or ducted intake/exhaust) and produce cool, dehumidified exhaust air — a closet install without ducting will underperform and may need a louvered door.
- Verify current TECH Clean CA / utility rebate availability before promising a rebate number to the client — as of early 2026 the statewide TECH Clean CA heat pump water heater rebate pool was reported fully reserved; LADWP customers may still have a separate local rebate (reported up to ~$2,500) — verify current spec/availability at time of sale.
