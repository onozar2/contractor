# Windows

## The ladder

**Good — Vinyl retrofit, mid-tier.** Milgard Style Line (V250, narrow frame profile, budget-friendly) or Simonton 5500/Reflections (3/4" insulating glass, ProSolar Low-E, argon fill, DP50 wind rating). Retrofit install (fits existing frame, no stucco cut) runs roughly **$700–$1,100/window installed**. Standard call for most existing-stucco replacements.

**Better — Vinyl retrofit, upgraded line.** Milgard Tuscany (V400, wider frame profile mimicking wood-window sightlines) or Anlin Catalina. Adds sound-dampening options, better hardware, broader color/grid choices. Roughly **$850–$1,300/window installed**.

**Best — Premium vinyl / reinforced.** Anlin Del Mar (Comfort-Core low-conductance foam-filled frame chambers, Innergy fiberglass reinforcement in sash/frame, 3-point Triple-Guard weatherstripping, factory sound package rated up to ~50% outside-noise reduction vs. standard dual-pane) or Simonton 5500 with StormBreaker impact-resistant option. New-construction install (stucco cut back, full flange, better rough-opening air-seal) runs **$1,000–$2,000+/window installed** — higher mainly due to stucco patch/paint, not the unit itself.

**Anlin Bay View / Panoramic** — slim-sightline, larger-glass series for view lots/modern remodels; a design-driven upgrade rather than a strict step. Note: could not verify an Anlin line called "Bay Pointe" or "Ventana" — verify current naming before quoting a client those names; confirmed current Anlin residential lines are Catalina, Del Mar, Bay View, and Panoramic.

## How to choose

- Existing stucco home, standard openings, budget-conscious → mid-tier retrofit (Style Line/Simonton 5500).
- Client mentions street noise, a boulevard, or a flight path (near LAX, Hollywood Burbank, major arterials) → step to Del Mar/upgraded sound package — the single best upgrade trigger.
- Full remodel/addition with stucco already open → new-construction install; premium line is easier to justify since the labor delta is smaller than on a retrofit-only job.
- View lots, hillside homes, great-room/ADU projects wanting large glass with minimal frame → Bay View/Panoramic.
- Fire-hardening or insurance-driven upgrades → confirm dual-pane minimum, and near wildland-urban interface (WUI) zones common in LA hillside areas, check with the local AHJ whether ignition-resistant glazing is required — jurisdiction-specific, verify current spec.

## Upsell notes

- Sound package (Del Mar): "This is the upgrade people notice in the first week — the house just feels quieter."
- Low-E + argon (standard tier): "This is what actually keeps the cooling bill down in summer, not just the frame."
- Reinforced frame: "Won't flex or bow in direct summer sun the way a thinner frame can over 15–20 years."
- Retrofit vs. new-construction: "If we're not opening the stucco, retrofit gets you 90% of the performance for a fraction of the mess and cost."

## Watch out

- Title 24 (2022 cycle) sets a prescriptive max U-factor of 0.30 and SHGC of 0.23 for residential fenestration across most CA climate zones, including the LA basin; a small-area exception (under 75 sq ft aggregate replacement) allows up to U-0.40/SHGC-0.35 — verify current spec against the code cycle in effect at permit time.
- Minimum dual-pane is required for Title 24 compliance — never quote single-pane as compliant.
- Triple-pane (Simonton 5500 option, ENERGY STAR "Most Efficient") adds real cost and weight — position for extreme noise/thermal cases, not as a default upsell; most LA jobs don't need it to hit Title 24.
- New-construction installs require stucco to be cut back and re-flashed — always scope patch/paint-match as a separate line item; texture mismatch is a common complaint if not set as an expectation up front.
- Many LA-area HOA guidelines restrict frame color (often white-only) or grid patterns — check CC&Rs before finalizing selections on HOA properties.
