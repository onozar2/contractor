# Garage Doors

## The ladder

**Door only, installed, single-car (double-car roughly 1.6-1.9x these numbers):**

- **Good — non-insulated steel:** $700-1,400/door installed. Flat or single-layer steel panel, no thermal break. Fine for a detached garage, workshop, or storage-only garage where the space isn't conditioned or lived-behind.
- **Better — insulated steel sandwich construction, R-6 to R-9:** $600-1,200/door installed (entry insulated tier — pricing overlaps the top of non-insulated because it's a base steel door with a foam layer added, not a premium line). Two steel skins with a polystyrene or thin polyurethane core. Cuts noise and some temperature transfer; the right call for an attached garage that isn't used as living/work space.
- **Better — insulated steel sandwich, R-12 to R-13:** $1,200-2,200/door installed. Thicker polyurethane-injected core (this is where Clopay's Canyon Ridge Elements-class and comparable Amarr mid-tier lines sit). Noticeably quieter door operation and better temperature control for a garage that backs up to a bedroom or is used as a workshop/gym.
- **Best — insulated steel sandwich, R-16 to R-18+, wood-look composite/faux-wood:** $2,000-4,000+/door installed. This is the Clopay Canyon Ridge (4-Layer, R-18.4) or Canyon Ridge 5-Layer (R-20.4)-class tier, or the comparable Amarr Thermacore wood-look line (up to ~R-19.4) — verify current model-year R-values before quoting, manufacturers revise these periodically. Composite overlay on steel gives the look of a carriage-house wood door (real wood grain texture, decorative hardware) without the warping, cracking, or repainting real wood requires. This is the curb-appeal tier — sell it on the house's street-facing design, not just insulation.

**Opener tiers, installed:**

- **Chain drive:** $200-400 installed. Loudest, least expensive, most durable for heavy/oversized doors. Fine for a detached garage or anyone not living near/above it.
- **Belt drive:** $300-550 installed. Rubber belt instead of metal chain — quiet enough for an attached garage below or beside a bedroom. This is the default recommendation for most attached-garage LA homes.
- **Jackshaft / wall-mount:** $700-1,200+ installed. Mounts beside the door on the wall instead of overhead on a rail — frees up the entire ceiling for storage, a car lift, or a shop layout, and is the standard choice when a client wants a clean, exposed-ceiling garage. Costs more because of the mounting hardware and torsion-shaft integration, not the drive mechanism itself.
- **Battery backup:** functionally not an upsell in California — see Watch out below. Frame it as included/required rather than optional.
- **Smart/Wi-Fi control (myQ, Chamberlain, LiftMaster app-based):** $50-150 added to any of the above tiers (often bundled into the opener itself on current-generation models). Remote open/close, close-confirmation alerts, scheduled auto-close, and camera add-ons on top-tier units.

## How to choose

- Detached garage, workshop, or storage-only structure with no living space nearby → non-insulated or entry insulated (R-6-R-9) steel, chain drive.
- Standard attached-garage tract home, garage below or beside a bedroom → mid-tier insulated (R-12-R-13) steel, belt drive, standard battery backup.
- Client mentions using the garage as a home gym, workshop, or hangout space → step up to R-16+ insulation regardless of door style — the insulation is doing real work there, not just marketing.
- House with strong street-facing curb appeal (corner lot, HOA community, higher-end block) or a client who's already upgrading the front elevation → wood-look composite door, R-16-R-18+.
- Client wants garage ceiling clear for storage racks, a car lift, or a shop buildout → jackshaft/wall-mount opener regardless of door tier.

## Upsell notes

"The insulated door isn't just about temperature — it's about noise. A single-layer steel door booms every time it opens or closes, and you'll hear that from inside the house. The insulated sandwich construction deadens that." For the wood-look composite step-up: "This gives you the exact look of a real wood carriage door — same grain, same panel detailing — but it'll never crack, split, or need repainting the way actual wood does, and it costs a fraction of what a true wood door runs." For jackshaft: "A normal opener eats your whole ceiling with a rail — this one mounts on the side wall, so you get the ceiling back for storage or a lift."

## Watch out

- California SB-969 requires battery backup on any garage door opener manufactured, sold, or installed in a California residence since July 1, 2019 — this is a hard legal requirement, not an upsell, and it applies to replacements: if you disconnect an old door from an opener that lacks battery backup and connect a new door, the opener must be brought up to compliance. Violations carry a $1,000 civil penalty. Always confirm the opener (new or existing) meets this before final signoff.
- Wind load: coastal-exposure and high-wind-zone properties (per ASCE 7 exposure category, determined by proximity to open coastline) may require a wind-load-rated door with reinforced struts — this is uncommon for most inland LA jobs but verify current requirement with the local building department for any coastal or hillside/ridge-exposed property before ordering a standard door.
- Existing opener compatibility: swapping in a heavier insulated door can exceed an older opener's lift capacity — always check opener horsepower/lift rating against the new door's weight, don't assume the old opener carries over.
- Track/header clearance: jackshaft and low-headroom track kits need specific header and side-room clearance that standard doors don't — measure before quoting a jackshaft opener on a garage with a low header or obstructed ceiling.
- HOA design review is common in LA planned communities for garage door style/color changes, especially wood-look composite doors that change the home's street-facing appearance — confirm before ordering.
- R-values and model-year specs shift between manufacturer refreshes — verify current spec (exact R-value, layer count, and pricing) against the live Clopay/Amarr catalog before quoting a specific model number to a customer.
