# Bathroom Vanities, Toilets & Fixtures

## The ladder

**Vanities:**
- *Good — stock vanity.* Big-box or distributor stock cabinet (24"-60" standard widths), laminate or thermofoil door, cultured marble or laminate top. Installed: roughly **$800-$2,000** for a 36" single vanity with top.
- *Better — semi-custom vanity.* Framed or frameless cabinet box with a choice of door styles/finishes/sizes from a dealer catalog, upgraded to a quartz or natural-stone top. Installed: roughly **$2,200-$4,500** for a 36"-48" single vanity with top.
- *Best — custom-built vanity.* Cabinetmaker-built to exact dimensions (furniture-style leg vanity, floating vanity, integrated double-sink run), solid wood or high-end finish, natural stone or quartz top, integrated sink or vessel bowls. Installed: **$5,000-$12,000+** depending on length and material.

**Toilets:**
- *Good — standard gravity-flush, WaterSense-certified.* American Standard Cadet 3, Kohler Highline. Reliable, low-cost, 1.28 gpf. Installed: **$375-$550**.
- *Better — high-efficiency gravity or single-flush comfort-height.* Kohler Cimarron, American Standard Champion 4, TOTO Drake — comfort/chair height (17"-19"), quieter flush, better bowl-wash. Installed: **$450-$700**.
- *Best — pressure-assist or premium one-piece skirted.* Kohler G-Force pressure-assist models (powerful, noisier) or TOTO/Kohler one-piece skirted-trapway designs (quiet, easy-clean smooth exterior), some with dual-flush. Installed: **$700-$1,200**; smart/bidet-integrated toilets (TOTO Washlet, Kohler Numi) run **$1,500-$5,000+** installed.

**Faucets & fixture finishes:**
- *Good — chrome, standard single-handle.* Builder-grade brand-name (Moen, Delta base lines). **$150-$350** installed per faucet.
- *Better — brushed nickel or matte black, mid-tier line.* **$300-$550** installed.
- *Best — brushed gold, oil-rubbed bronze, or designer brand (Kohler Purist, Delta Trinsic, California Faucets).* **$500-$900+** installed per faucet; matching towel bars/robe hooks/shower trim as a full "finish package" adds **$400-$900** more.

## How to choose

- Rental, flip, or fast-turn budget remodel → stock vanity, standard WaterSense toilet, chrome fixtures.
- Typical owner-occupied full bath remodel → semi-custom vanity with quartz top, comfort-height toilet, brushed nickel or matte black fixtures — this is the most-sold tier in LA remodels.
- Primary suite or double-vanity master bath → custom vanity run, premium one-piece toilet, matched designer finish package across faucet/shower trim/hardware for a cohesive look.
- Households with mobility concerns or aging-in-place → comfort-height toilet is close to mandatory regardless of budget tier; consider pressure-assist for households with well-worn plumbing (better clog resistance).
- Client fixated on water/utility bills → lead with WaterSense-certified options at every tier; it's a genuine selling point, not just a code checkbox.

## Upsell notes

"A stock vanity comes in standard sizes — if your wall isn't exactly that width you're stuck with wasted space or a squeeze. A custom build uses every inch." "Comfort height means you're not doing a mini-squat every time — it's the same seat height as a normal chair." "Matte black or brushed gold fixtures cost more, but it's the one thing everyone touches and sees every single day — it's what makes the room feel finished versus just functional." "A skirted one-piece toilet has no crevices around the base to trap grime — you'll actually clean it less."

## Watch out

Comfort-height toilets (17"-19") don't fit every layout — verify clearance to the door swing and adjacent fixtures before quoting, especially in older, narrower SoCal bathrooms. Vessel sinks and floating vanities need blocking in the wall for support — flag this at framing stage, not after drywall. Pressure-assist toilets are louder (a real complaint in single-story open-plan homes) — mention this trade-off, don't just sell "more powerful." Verify current spec/pricing before quoting — toilet and fixture MSRPs shift with brand promotions and supply. CALGreen mandates WaterSense-labeled fixtures on any permitted bathroom remodel in California — never quote a non-WaterSense toilet on a permitted job.
