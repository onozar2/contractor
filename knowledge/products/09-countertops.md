# Countertops

Countertop tiers — the countertop options ladder: laminate → butcher block → quartz countertop tiers (2cm vs 3cm) → granite → porcelain slab → Dekton, with LA-market installed price-per-sf ranges and edge-profile upsells.

## The ladder

**Good — Laminate:** ~$20-45/sf installed. Formica-class laminate — least expensive, widest color range, but seams show and it can't take direct heat or heavy cutting. Butcher block/wood sits at a similar or slightly higher price point (~$35-70/sf installed) and is a popular island accent rather than a whole-kitchen surface in most LA remodels — warm look, but needs regular oiling and isn't heat/scratch proof.

**Better — Quartz (engineered stone), 2cm or 3cm:** ~$50-150/sf installed; most LA jobs land $70-100/sf. Non-porous, no sealing required, consistent pattern (better for slab matching/seams than natural stone). **2cm** is the lighter, more affordable option but needs a plywood substrate underneath for support (adds $5-8/sf back in material/labor). **3cm** costs roughly 15-30% more in material but skips the substrate — net difference to the client is often smaller than the raw material premium suggests, and 3cm gives the thicker, more substantial-looking edge homeowners tend to want on an island.

Granite runs a comparable or wider range (~$40-175/sf installed) depending on rarity of the slab — a fine "better" or "best" option for clients who want a natural-stone look with more heat tolerance than quartz.

**Best — Porcelain slab or Dekton:** Porcelain (Neolith-class) ~$55-120/sf installed, national average $75-95/sf. **Dekton** (Cosentino's sintered stone) ~$55-150/sf, averaging around $95/sf — roughly 10-20% more than comparable porcelain and 20-40% more than quartz, but with the best scratch/heat/UV resistance of any option here, and it can go outdoors (BBQ islands, covered patios) where quartz can't. Both are ultra-thin-capable for waterfall edges and large-format seamless runs.

## How to choose

- Rental/flip or a client on a tight budget → laminate; fastest, cheapest, does the job.
- Standard owner-occupied kitchen remodel, most common LA choice → quartz, 2cm for budget-conscious, 3cm for islands or a heavier look.
- Client wants a natural, one-of-a-kind stone look and understands sealing maintenance → granite.
- Outdoor kitchen, BBQ island, or heavy indoor-outdoor LA living space → Dekton (UV-stable, handles direct sun and heat) or porcelain.
- Design-forward client wanting large seamless islands or a waterfall edge with minimal visible seams → porcelain or Dekton in large-format slabs.

## Upsell notes

"Quartz never needs sealing and won't stain from wine or lemon juice sitting on it — granite looks great but you're resealing it every year or two." On 3cm: "The thicker edge just reads as more substantial when you walk into the kitchen — it's the difference between looking like a rental and looking finished." On Dekton/porcelain: "This is the only counter on this list you can put outside by the grill without worrying about sun fading or heat damage."

**Edge profile as upsell (all materials):** eased/pencil edge is standard and included; step up to **bullnose** (fully rounded, softer look, kid-safe corners) or **ogee** (decorative S-curve, more traditional/formal) for a moderate labor upcharge; **waterfall edge** (slab wraps down the side of an island to the floor) is the premium statement upsell — it roughly doubles the slab material needed for that run (since the "side panel" is full-height slab, not just a thin edge build-up) and is priced as a separate line item, not a percentage add.

## Watch out

- 2cm quartz requires a plywood substrate — confirm this is priced in, since some low quotes omit it and then change-order it in later.
- Natural stone (granite) needs periodic resealing; set that maintenance expectation at time of sale so it doesn't become a callback complaint.
- Dekton/porcelain fabrication requires specialized tooling and Dekton-certified fabricators — not every countertop shop in LA can cut it; confirm fabricator capability before quoting, and verify current spec on turnaround time since it can run longer than quartz/granite.
- Overhangs on islands (especially with seating) need corbels or steel support brackets per most fabricator warranties once the unsupported overhang exceeds roughly 10-12"; confirm with the fabricator's engineering spec rather than guessing, since this affects both cost and code/safety.
- Undermount sinks require the edge/reveal detail to be decided before templating — this is a decision point to lock in at the countertop selection meeting, not after.
