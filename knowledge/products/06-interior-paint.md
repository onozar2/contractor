# Interior Paint

## The ladder

**Sheen by room (applies at every price tier):**
- Ceilings: flat/matte — hides drywall imperfections, no scrubbing needed.
- Living areas, bedrooms, hallways: eggshell (most common) or matte for a cleaner, more current look on smooth walls.
- Kitchens, bathrooms, laundry: satin — enough sheen to wipe down grease/moisture without looking glossy.
- Trim, doors, cabinets: semi-gloss (or ProClassic-type enamel) — durable, wipeable, and it visually separates trim from wall color.

**Brand/tier ladder, installed (labor + material, LA market, per gallon of paint as the reference unit — a typical 3-bed/2-bath repaint runs 8-14 gallons):**

- **Good — Behr Premium Plus** (Home Depot): ~$28-33/gal retail. Paint-and-primer-in-one, fine for low-traffic rooms, rentals, or a fast flip. Dunn-Edwards **Spartawall** (~$30-40/gal contractor price) is the pro-channel equivalent — zero/ultra-low VOC, standard sheen ladder, built for repaint volume.
- **Better — Sherwin-Williams SuperPaint** (~$55-65/gal) or **Behr Marquee** (~$50-55/gal, one-coat hide guarantee). This is the workhorse tier for most owner-occupied jobs — better hide, better washability, holds color longer than Premium Plus.
- **Best — Sherwin-Williams Emerald** (~$95-116/gal) or **Duration** (~$80-90/gal), or Dunn-Edwards **Suprema** (~$60/gal contractor price, ultra-low VOC, exceptional hide). For trim/doors/cabinets, step up to **ProClassic waterborne enamel** (~$85-95/gal) regardless of which wall-paint tier the client picks — it's the industry-standard trim finish for a hard, furniture-like cure.

Whole-house repaint labor (LA, 2-3 bed, walls + ceilings + trim, not including cabinet paint): roughly $4,500-$9,000 depending on prep (patching, popcorn-ceiling texture matching, high ceilings) and tier of paint specified.

## How to choose

- Rental/investment property or pre-sale flip → Premium Plus / Spartawall tier; nobody's living with it long-term.
- Owner-occupied family home, normal traffic → SuperPaint/Marquee/Suprema tier — the sweet spot most LA homeowners land on.
- High-traffic households (kids, pets, open-concept kitchen-to-living flow) or anyone who's repainted the same wall three times in five years → Emerald/Duration/ProClassic — worth the premium in fewer repaint cycles.
- Dark or saturated color changes over existing color → budget for the better-hide lines (Marquee, Emerald, Suprema) regardless of tier — cheap paint means a third coat, which erases any savings.
- Cabinets, trim, doors → always spec an enamel (ProClassic-class), never a wall paint, no matter what tier the walls get.

## Upsell notes

"The cheap paint needs two, sometimes three coats to look right — the better stuff covers in two and actually resists your kids' hands and the dog rubbing against it at nose height. You're paying once instead of touching this up every 18 months." For kitchens/baths: "Satin or better sheens actually wipe clean — flat paint in a kitchen turns into a stain magnet within a year."

## Watch out

- New/bare drywall always needs a PVA drywall primer first (not paint-and-primer-in-one) — skipping it causes flashing (uneven sheen) and wastes finish coats.
- Water stains, smoke, pet odor, or old nicotine require a shellac-based stain-blocking primer (Zinsser BIN-class) — regular primer will not stop bleed-through.
- Painting over existing semi-gloss/glossy trim or oil-based paint requires a bonding primer (Zinsser 1-2-3 Plus/STIX-class) after scuff-sanding, or the new coat will peel.
- Low-VOC/zero-VOC is not optional messaging in LA — SCAQMD Rule 1113 caps VOC content for architectural coatings sold in the South Coast Air Basin; all the tiers above comply, but verify current spec on any specialty/oil-based product before ordering.
- Popcorn ceilings: confirm no asbestos testing is needed for pre-1980s construction before any scraping/prep work is proposed.
