# Kitchen Appliances & Fixtures

## The ladder

**Appliances (full kitchen suite — range, fridge, dishwasher, hood/microwave — installed):**

- **Good — Builder-grade:** Frigidaire, GE (standard, non-Café lines), or comparable. A full suite typically lands well under $10,000 installed. Reliable, standard-capacity, gets the job done — this is what's in most tract-home kitchens and what a landlord or flip specifies.
- **Better — Mid-tier:** KitchenAid, Bosch, GE Café. Better build quality, quieter dishwashers, more finish/handle options (Café in particular is popular for its customizable hardware color). A high-end KitchenAid or Bosch counter-depth fridge alone can run $2,500-5,000; a full suite typically lands in the $8,000-18,000 range depending on models chosen. This is the sweet spot for most LA remodels — real quality step-up without professional-grade pricing.
- **Best — Premium/professional:** Thermador, Wolf, Sub-Zero, Viking. Sub-Zero refrigeration alone runs roughly $11,600-20,400+; Wolf and Thermador built-in ranges/ovens run $7,000-14,000 per unit. A full Sub-Zero/Wolf or Thermador suite easily runs $25,000-50,000+. Wolf is generally the safer/more broadly recommended pro brand; Thermador is often the best value at this tier; Viking suits buyers who specifically want higher BTU cooking output. Sub-Zero is the standout at the very top for refrigeration specifically (dual-compressor design).

**Faucets & sinks (per fixture, installed):**

- **Good:** Delta entry-level (from ~$100) or Moen entry-level (under ~$170). Fine, functional, standard finishes.
- **Better:** Moen or Delta mid-range ($200-400) — this is where both brands offer the best value; Delta adds smart features (Touch2O, VoiceIQ) and a much wider model selection (9,000+ SKUs vs Moen's ~200).
- **Best:** Kohler premium ($400+) — best-in-class finish quality and design detail; Kohler's advantage is minimal in the budget/mid tiers but clearly shows at the premium end. Delta's smart/touchless faucets run $700+ at the top.

## How to choose

- Rental, flip, or investor-owned property → builder-grade (Frigidaire/GE) suite; matches the price point of the sale/lease.
- Standard owner-occupied kitchen remodel, most common LA client → mid-tier (KitchenAid/Bosch/Café) — real quality gain most homeowners actually notice day to day (quieter dishwasher, better fridge organization).
- Client who cooks seriously or entertains often and has the budget → premium (Wolf/Thermador range + Sub-Zero fridge) — the performance and prestige difference is real and visible to guests.
- Faucet choice should roughly track the appliance tier and the sink/countertop investment — a $150 faucet on a $10,000 quartz island reads as an afterthought.
- Smart/touchless faucet features are a genuine convenience upsell for households with young kids or people who cook with messy hands often — worth mentioning regardless of budget tier.

## Upsell notes

"Bosch and KitchenAid dishwashers run noticeably quieter — you can run it during dinner and not raise your voice." On premium: "A Wolf range and a Sub-Zero fridge aren't just about cooking better, they're what buyers expect to see in a listing photo when you eventually list this house — appraisers and agents both notice the brand names." On faucets: "Touchless means you're not smearing raw chicken on the handle — it pays for itself the first time you're mid-recipe with messy hands."

## Watch out

- Professional-grade ranges (Wolf, Viking) often need a higher BTU gas line and a dedicated make-up air / higher-CFM ventilation hood to meet code — confirm gas line sizing and venting before quoting a pro range, this is a common change-order surprise.
- Sub-Zero and other built-in/panel-ready refrigerators typically require custom cabinetry openings with specific clearances (and sometimes panel-ready doors ordered separately) — this needs to be coordinated with the cabinet order, not decided after cabinets are built.
- LA/California title 24 and local utility rebate programs sometimes affect which dishwasher/range models qualify for rebates — worth checking current program specifics before promising a rebate to the client; verify current spec.
- Gas range/cooktop clients should confirm whether the city/permit requires anti-tip bracket installation and, where relevant, code compliance for gas shutoff valves — inspectors do check this in LA.
- Matching finish (stainless, panel-ready, or matte black/slate) across the full suite matters visually — confirm the client wants a consistent finish before ordering piecemeal from different tiers or brands.
