# Roofing: Shingles + Underlayment

## The ladder

**Good — 3-tab asphalt.** GAF Royal Sovereign (~180 lbs/sq, 25-yr warranty, 15-yr 60-mph wind, 10-yr StainGuard algae) or CertainTeed XT 25 (25-yr warranty, 10-yr algae, 5-yr wind — verify current wind spec). Installed: **$320–$775/square**, most SoCal jobs $450–600. Mostly flip/rental-grade now; some suppliers are de-stocking 3-tab.

**Better — Architectural (dimensional) laminate.** GAF Timberline HDZ (GAF's #1 seller; lifetime/50-yr non-prorated warranty, 25-yr StainGuard Plus algae, 15-yr WindProven no-max-wind warranty with 4 qualifying accessories) or CertainTeed Landmark (~230 lbs/sq — verify current spec, 25-yr algae warranty). Installed: **$600–$900/square**. Default recommendation for most LA reroofs.

**Best — Presidential/luxury laminate.** GAF Timberline UHDZ (20% thicker look, Dual Shadow Line, 30-yr StainGuard Plus PRO algae) or CertainTeed Landmark Pro (270 lbs/sq, Class 3 impact rated, 24 Max Definition colors, 30-yr algae) / Landmark Premium (300 lbs/sq) / Presidential Shake TL (triple-layer wood-shake profile, Class 4 impact rated, 110-mph standard wind, upgradable to 130). Installed: **$900–$1,400+/square**.

## How to choose

- Flip/rental reroof, price-only → 3-tab, but confirm supplier stock first.
- Standard single-family, owner staying 10+ years → HDZ or Landmark, the default "better" pitch.
- Hillside/high-value areas (Palisades, Malibu-adjacent, Hollywood Hills) or shake look without tile weight → Presidential Shake TL or Landmark Premium.
- Wildfire-adjacent lots or insurance pushing impact resistance → Landmark Pro/Premium or UHDZ, lead with Class 3/4 rating.
- Client mentions hail claims or nonrenewal notice → impact-rated tiers can help insurability, not just durability.

## Upsell notes

- "Same shingle family, built thicker — won't look flat and papery from the street like the basic version."
- "Longer algae coverage means it won't get that gray-black streaking other roofs in the neighborhood pick up after a few years."
- Impact-rated: "Rated to take a hit without cracking — some insurers give a credit for that."
- GAF WindProven (needs 4 qualifying accessories): "It's the whole system, not just the shingle, that makes the no-wind-limit warranty valid."

## Watch out

- CRRC (Cool Roof Rating Council) listing is required for Title 24 compliance on qualifying re-roofs — verify the specific color/product is CRRC-rated, not just the product line.
- LA basin spans Title 24 Climate Zones 6, 8, and 9 depending on distance from the coast; steep-slope cool-roof minimums (~0.20 aged solar reflectance, 0.75 thermal emittance) apply prescriptively in certain zones only — verify current spec/zone by address.
- Top warranty tiers (GAF Golden Pledge, CertainTeed 5-Star) require certified installers and specific accessory packages — don't promise them unless the crew is certified.
- Underlayment ladder: entry synthetic (GAF FeltBuster/Tiger Paw, ASTM D226/D4869, ~180-day UV) vs. premium breathable (GAF Deck-Armor, 3.7 lb/100 sq ft) vs. self-adhered peel-and-stick (GAF WeatherWatch or StormGuard, high-temp rated ~250°F) at valleys/eaves/low-slope. Plain 15-lb felt is effectively obsolete on quality LA jobs.
- "UDL 25/30/50" is an Owens Corning Titanium product, not GAF — don't label it "GAF UDL" on a GAF-shingle proposal; use GAF's own underlayment names to keep the system warranty intact.
