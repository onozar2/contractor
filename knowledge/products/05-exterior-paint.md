# Exterior Paint & Coatings

## The ladder

**Good — Standard 100% acrylic, wash-and-paint.** Sherwin-Williams SuperPaint (15-yr warranty, ~350–400 sq ft/gallon coverage — verify current price, SW pricing moves seasonally) over pressure-wash-only prep. Best for sound existing paint with no active cracking. Installed roughly **$1.50–$4/sq ft**, or **$3,000–$8,000** for a typical 2,000 sq ft stucco home, color-coat only.

**Better — Premium acrylic + real prep.** Sherwin-Williams Duration (lifetime limited warranty, PermaLast additive makes the film ~70% thicker, self-priming/one-coat on most previously-painted surfaces) paired with scrape-and-spot-prime — sanding failing areas, spot-priming bare wood/stucco, caulking gaps before color coat. Installed roughly **$3–$6/sq ft**, or **$6,000–$12,000** for a 2,000 sq ft home depending on condition.

**Best — Elastomeric or ceramic "cool wall" systems + full stucco patch.** Two distinct premium paths:
- **Elastomeric** (true waterproofing membrane, bridges hairline stucco cracks, tested to ~98-mph wind-driven rain): lower coverage (~100 sq ft/gallon vs. 250–300 for acrylic), so material cost per sq ft is higher. Installed roughly **$1.50–$5/sq ft**, $3,000–$8,500 for 2,000 sq ft. Right call on bare/never-coated stucco or active hairline cracking.
- **Tex-Cote CoolWall** (ceramic-microsphere heat-reflective coating, applied ~10x thicker than standard paint, DOE Oak Ridge-tested to cut cooling costs up to ~21.9% and wall surface temps up to 40°F vs. dark traditional paint): installed roughly **$6.50–$12.50/sq ft**, $6,000–$18,000 for 2,000 sq ft. A genuine step above premium acrylic — sell on energy performance, not just paint quality.

Full stucco patch/re-texture (cracks, spalling, chip repair) is a separate line item on any tier — it's substrate repair, not a paint-grade upsell.

## How to choose

- Newer home (under 30–40 yrs), existing acrylic in decent shape → Duration over SuperPaint; self-priming/one-coat performance pays for itself in labor.
- Older home, especially with cedar trim or wood needing to breathe → stay in acrylic (elastomeric only on masonry, never wood trim — it traps moisture and blisters).
- Bare/never-coated stucco or visible hairline cracking → elastomeric, sold specifically as waterproofing.
- Client mentions high cooling bills, west-facing walls, or unshaded stucco baking in LA summer sun → CoolWall/ceramic tier, framed as an energy-cost conversation.
- Home with failing/peeling elastomeric already on it → flag as prep-intensive (elastomeric adheres poorly over degraded elastomeric); may need mechanical removal before recoat, price accordingly.

## Upsell notes

- Duration vs. SuperPaint: "Self-priming and goes on thicker in one coat — it holds up through more LA summers before it needs touching up."
- Elastomeric: "This actually seals hairline cracks instead of just covering them — water can't get behind the stucco anymore."
- CoolWall/ceramic: "This measurably drops your wall surface temperature — people notice it in the cooling bill, especially on the west-facing side."
- Full prep vs. wash-only: "Paint only lasts as long as what's underneath it — skipping this step is why some repaints start peeling again in two or three years."

## Watch out

- Elastomeric won't adhere well over stucco with multiple prior paint coats, and it's less breathable than acrylic — trapped moisture behind it on an already-multi-coated wall can cause blistering/peeling; a real compatibility risk, not just an upsell point.
- CoolWall/ceramic systems are proprietary (specific prep/primer/coat sequence) — installer must be factory-trained/certified to keep the manufacturer warranty valid; don't promise the branded warranty unless the crew is certified.
- Many LA-area HOA guidelines restrict exterior color palettes to an approved list — confirm before color selection, especially since darker colors reduce CoolWall's reflective performance.
- Elastomeric and CoolWall both carry real fade/discoloration risk on darker custom colors under intense UV — set expectations on dark color choices regardless of tier.
- "Lifetime" warranties (Duration, some CoolWall dealer warranties) are typically limited/prorated and tied to proper prep and a certified applicator — check the actual warranty document before repeating "lifetime" as a guarantee.
