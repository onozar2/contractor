# Flooring: Wood, Laminate & Vinyl Plank

## The ladder

- *Good — laminate, AC3 rating, or SPC luxury vinyl plank with 12-20 mil wear layer.* Photographic wood-look layer over a fiberboard (laminate) or rigid stone-composite (SPC) core, floating click-lock install over existing subfloor. AC3 handles all-residential heavy traffic (kitchens, hallways). Installed: roughly **$4-$7 per sq ft**.
- *Better — engineered hardwood, or WPC/SPC LVP with 20 mil wear layer.* Engineered hardwood is a real hardwood wear layer (typically 2-6mm) bonded over a plywood core — can be nailed, glued, or floated, and can usually be refinished once or twice. 20 mil LVP is the sweet spot for households with kids and pets. Installed: engineered hardwood **$8-$14 per sq ft**; 20 mil SPC/WPC **$6-$9 per sq ft**.
- *Best — solid hardwood, or commercial-grade 28+ mil LVP.* Solid hardwood (site-finished oak, white oak, hickory) is nailed to a plywood subfloor, sanded and finished on-site, and can be refinished multiple times over its life (50+ years). Installed: **$12-$20+ per sq ft**. 28+ mil LVP is the highest-durability vinyl tier — rated for commercial and heavy-pet/high-traffic residential use. Installed: **$8-$12 per sq ft**.

## How to choose

- Rental or flip, tight budget, fast turnaround → AC3 laminate or 12 mil SPC — durable enough for residential turnover, cheapest per-foot cost.
- Family with kids, dogs, or a busy kitchen/entry → 20 mil SPC/WPC LVP; it's 100% waterproof (unlike laminate or hardwood) and shrugs off scratches better than laminate's photo layer.
- Bathroom, laundry room, or any room with real water exposure → SPC over WPC (SPC's rigid stone core handles moisture and temperature swings better) — never laminate or solid hardwood in a genuinely wet room.
- Client wants "real wood" resale value / higher-end listing → engineered hardwood over a slab (can't nail solid hardwood to concrete) or solid hardwood over a raised wood subfloor.
- Multi-generational or long-term-owned home where refinishing decades from now matters → solid hardwood is the only option that can be sanded/refinished multiple times; engineered can usually go once, LVP/laminate never.

## Upsell notes

"Laminate is a photograph of wood under a plastic wear layer — LVP with a 20 mil layer is thick enough that a dog's nails or a dropped pan won't punch through to the print underneath." "Real hardwood is the one floor you can sand down and refinish again in 15 years if it gets beat up — everything else in this room, you replace." "SPC core doesn't expand and contract with humidity the way wood does, so it's the safer call for a slab foundation or anywhere near the kitchen sink."

## Watch out

Solid hardwood cannot go directly over a concrete slab (common in SoCal single-story additions and newer construction) — needs a raised subfloor or engineered hardwood instead; flag this immediately on slab homes. Engineered and solid hardwood both need an acclimation period on-site (typically several days) before install — factor this into the schedule. AC ratings apply to laminate only — don't quote an "AC rating" on LVP, use mil wear-layer thickness instead; these are different scales and mixing them up in front of a client reads as unprepared. SPC is harder/louder underfoot and less forgiving than WPC, which has a foam-backed core that's quieter and slightly softer — mention this if the client is sensitive to noise (upstairs rooms, condos with HOA sound rules). Verify current mil/AC specs and brand-specific warranty terms before quoting a named product line — manufacturers revise wear-layer offerings frequently.
