# Fence & Gate Materials — Factory-Direct (imported) vs Domestic Wholesale

Outdoor **gate and fence** materials, sourced factory-direct (containers from Asia) vs
bought from a US domestic wholesaler. Material cost only — not installed. Every price is
tagged **[SUB]** (given to Ori directly by a subcontractor/supplier who imports
factory-direct) or **[ONLINE]** (web research: Alibaba/Made-in-China listings, US
distributor listings, CBP/Federal Register tariff filings). As of 2026-07-13.

> **Provenance note:** the four headline numbers (Vinyl 40–55, Composite 65, Aluminum 75,
> Wood 55) came **[SUB]** — from a fencing/gate sub who buys direct from overseas factories.
> The specific sub's name is to be confirmed by Ori; attach it to these entries. Everything
> labeled **[ONLINE]** is independent validation, not from the sub.

## First: what a "box" is (this was the key ambiguity)

A "box" = **one pre-assembled fence PANEL section**, not a bulk carton of loose footage.
Validated **[ONLINE]** against a real Alibaba packaging spec (one vinyl carton = 253 cm ≈
8.3 ft long, 35 kg = a single 8-ft panel). Panel widths differ by material:

- **Vinyl & wood panels ≈ 8 ft wide → ~8 linear feet per box.**
- **Aluminum & composite panels ≈ 6 ft wide → ~6 linear feet per box.**

This matters for the per-foot math. **Always ask the supplier to confirm LF-per-box** —
some factories price per panel/set, some per linear foot, with no consistent labeling.

- **[SUB]** $40–55 per box = per 8-ft privacy panel. In stock (port clear). → **≈ $5.00–$6.88
  per linear foot** of material. The sub imports direct, so this is already a landed,
  pass-through price.
- **[ONLINE] Factory FOB** (Alibaba/Made-in-China): $23–35/panel economy, $56–63/panel

- **[ONLINE] If Joon imported directly:** landed ≈ **$4.50–$11.50/LF** after ~40% duty +

- **[ONLINE] Domestic wholesale:** $9.17–20/LF for 6-ft privacy panels (economy Boulder

- **Excel check:** the model's Vinyl row ($47.5 box, $5.94/LF = box ÷ 8) is **VALIDATED** —
  ÷8 is correct because a vinyl panel is 8 ft wide. Its domestic figure directionally holds too.

## Aluminum

- **[SUB]** $75 per box (~6-ft powder-coated panel). Out of stock (at port). → **~$12.50/LF**
  (not $9.38 — see Excel check).
- **[ONLINE] Domestic wholesale:** $11.84–$40.84/LF (Home Depot Forgeright $11.84/LF;
  FenceWorkshop 6-ft panels $80–245; Barrette $28–29/LF). So the sub's $75/panel ≈ a comparable
  domestic 6-ft panel ($71–100).
- **[ONLINE] Import reality — the important finding:** Chinese aluminum fence is inside the
  **Antidumping/Countervailing Duty order on aluminum extrusions**: AD 86% China-wide + CVD
  7.4% + **Section 232 aluminum 50%** + Section 301 ~25%. Stacking AD + Sec.232 alone MORE
  THAN DOUBLES landed cost over FOB. **Importing aluminum fence direct from China does not
  pencil out.** That the sub's "factory-direct" aluminum lands at ~domestic price corroborates
  this — the duties eat the discount.
- **Verdict: do NOT import aluminum direct.** Buy domestically or from the sub at
  domestic-parity, whichever is more reliable on availability.
- **Excel check:** Excel's ÷8 ($9.38/LF) **understates** aluminum — a 6-ft panel is ÷6 →
  ~$12.50/LF. And the Excel implies a big import saving ($75 vs $160 domestic) that the
  tariff stack erases — **misleading on aluminum.**

## Duties, freight & lead time (why the Excel understated landed cost)

The Excel compared the FOB box price straight to a domestic price and **skipped duties +
freight entirely.** Real landed = FOB + duty + ocean freight + drayage + broker.

- **Duty [ONLINE]:** vinyl & composite ~**40.3%** (5.3% MFN + 25% Sec.301 + 10% Sec.122; HTS
  3925.90). Aluminum far higher (86%+ AD/CVD + 50% Sec.232). **These rates moved 3+ times in
  the last year** (SCOTUS struck the IEEPA tariffs Feb 2026; Sec.122 10% may expire ~July 24,
  2026) — confirm live with a customs broker before ordering.
- **Freight [ONLINE]:** $2,100–2,700 per 40ft early 2026, but **$6,000–7,400 at the current
  July peak.** ~3,500 LF (vinyl) / ~1,100–1,300 LF (composite) per 40ft container.
- **Lead time [ONLINE]:** ~45–65 days (vinyl), ~42–70 days (composite), ~8–14 weeks
  (aluminum, with AD/CVD entry scrutiny) door-to-door. The sub's "waiting for containers to be
  released from the port" = the customs/drayage stage — normal, 3–10 days when not flagged.

## When should JOON import directly (vs buy through the sub)?

Only vinyl and composite are even candidates (aluminum tariffs kill it; wood is domestic).
Break-even is roughly **2–3+ containers/year (~7,000–12,000+ LF/yr)** — below that, the
fixed costs (freight forwarder, customs broker, duty filing, QC risk on an unseen factory,
warehousing) eat the FOB discount. **For now, buying vinyl/composite through the sub who
already imports is the better play** — he carries the container risk, MOQ, and customs, and
still passes through near-factory pricing.
