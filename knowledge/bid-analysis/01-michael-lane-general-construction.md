## Payment schedule
- Deposit: 10%, up to $1,000
- Material Order (if custom windows/cabinets etc.) or Start of Work: 30%
- Completion of Rough: 35%
- Substantial Completion of Line Item (e.g., tile done, water/lights connected): 25%
- Last balance due at Walk Through, post-punch-list: up to 10%

**Flag:** these five stages sum to 110% (10+30+35+25+10), not 100% — an apparent drafting error in the source document. Do not treat as a template to copy verbatim; the *staging logic* (deposit → material/start → rough → substantial completion → final holdback) is sound, but the percentages need correcting to sum to 100% in any derivative template.

## What this teaches
A strong example of **itemized, priced-per-scope bidding** (vs. one lump sum) — useful structural pattern for multi-trade jobs where the client wants to see cost broken out per room/system. Also a cautionary example: the "Ground Up Builders" mismatch and the 110%-summing payment schedule show the risk of copy-pasting boilerplate between company templates without updating it.
