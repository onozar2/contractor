## Inclusions / exclusions — coverage split
**Client-supplied (explicit list):** "all finish materials. such as tile, faucets, sinks, doors, windows, tankless water heater, mirrors, wall sconces or any other decorative items, unless specifically indicated as to be provided by contractor." Kitchen countertop material and bathroom vanity/toilet/sink are called out a second time as customer-provided within the scope itself.

**Contractor-covered:** roof tear-off/replacement, gutters, skylight framing, drywall repair, tile *installation* (material implicitly customer's unless stated), kitchen/bath plumbing labor, mini-split install, bathroom demo/waterproofing/electrical/paint labor.

**Notable carve-out:** the kitchen bench/cabinet is entirely outside the contractor's scope — customer contracts and pays a separate cabinet maker directly. This is a useful pattern for "referred-trade, not our scope" language.

**Permits/fees — client pays:** "responsible for all building permit fees, engineer/any other city fees, inspection fees, engineering fees, and other public fees ... HERS raters & structural observation fees in case requested by building & safety inspector."

**Other clauses:**
- No work outside the property line.
- Timeline: up to 3 months or less from start (excludes holidays/weekends/weather) — notably longer than the 45-day bathroom-only bids, consistent with the broader multi-trade scope (roof + kitchen + bath + AC).
- Reimburse-contractor-for-materials-purchased-on-client's-behalf clause repeated verbatim from bid #02.
- Standard installations (tile, floor covering, moldings) must comply with code.

## What this teaches
Same Guideline Builders boilerplate exclusions paragraph as bids #02, #05, #06, #07 — reinforces that this is their standard house language, strong signal for what the Joon template's default exclusions section should say. The "customer pays a specialty vendor (cabinet maker) directly, outside our scope" carve-out is a useful optional clause for the Joon template when Joon doesn't want to carry markup/liability on a client-preferred vendor.
