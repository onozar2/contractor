# How Joon prices a project — pricing explained

## Why two contractor quotes for the "same job" can be $20K apart

Most quote gaps come from scope, not margin. One bid includes rough materials,
haul-away, permits coordination, and patch-and-paint; the other quietly excludes
them. Joon's Scope of Work names every inclusion and exclusion per trade so
bids can be compared like-for-like.

## What a Joon price always includes

- Labor for every step written in the Scope of Work, performed by licensed,
  vetted subcontractors under Joon's supervision.
- Rough materials (framing lumber, drywall, cement, waterproofing, copper/ABS,
  wire, fasteners) unless the scope says otherwise.
- Demolition, daily site protection, cleanup, haul-away, and dump fees.
- Coordination of inspections for permitted work.
- Sales tax on materials Joon supplies.

## What is typically NOT included (and why)

- **Finish materials the client selects** — tile, slabs, plumbing fixtures,
  lighting, appliances, vanities. You pick them; we set an allowance so the
  budget stays honest either way.
- **Permit and city fees** — set by the city, paid at cost, not marked up.
- **Engineering, surveys, soil reports** — third-party professionals billed at
  face value.
- **Hidden-condition repairs** — dry rot, non-code wiring, slab cracks found
  after demo are priced by written change order before any extra work begins.

## How the number is built

1. Measure everything on the walkthrough — price is built from quantities, not
   a guess per room.
2. Each trade's scope is priced from Joon's price book (blended from our logged
   jobs and current SoCal benchmarks).
3. Allowances are stated per selection item so client choices move the price
   transparently.
4. The payment schedule follows California law: deposit capped at the lesser of
   10% or $1,000, progress payments tied to completed stages, and the final
   balance due only at walk-through.

## Questions clients should ask any contractor

- Is the deposit legal? (CA caps it at 10% or $1,000, whichever is less.)
- Does the price include haul-away, rough materials, and patch/paint?
- What exactly is excluded, in writing?
- How are hidden conditions priced after demo?
- Do payment stages total exactly 100% of the contract price?
