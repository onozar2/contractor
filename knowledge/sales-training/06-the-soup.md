## Process & contract rules (the 9-step structure)

1. **Connection** — build rapport before anything else:
   - Find something in common between you and the client.
   - Give compliments.
   - Smile.
   - Physical connection: hug, handshake/kiss (cultural greeting), tap on the shoulder.
   - Mental connection: look good, smell good, dress good (i.e., the salesman's own presentation).

2. **Building a Strategy** — tell the client about 2-3 projects you're doing in the neighborhood, for three explicit reasons:
   - (A) To show them you're a local contractor.
   - (B) To help you with the rebuttals/loops at closing later.
   - (C) To use as leverage if the client wants to cancel.

3. **Measurements** — take measurements to show the client how you arrived at the price. If the client wants the price right away (skipping rapport), start with measurements as the entry point instead.

4. **Build the Product** — explain the product/scope to the client in extreme detail.

5. **Build Yourself and the Company** — establish personal and company credibility using two parallel lists:
   - Personal: family man, time in the business, giving to charity, successful, smart, religious, no drinking or gambling.
   - Company: time in the business, licensed/bonded/insured/workers' comp, good reviews, big company, big projects, warranties, family business, return clients, "apples to apples" guarantee.

6. **Stories** — **75% of the time you speak must be in stories.** (Exact whiteboard quote: "75% of the time you speak must be in stories.")

7. **Cash vs. Finance** — how the two payment paths are framed differently: cash is quoted as a total price; finance is quoted in payments. (Exact whiteboard quote: "cash in total price and finance in payments.")

8. **Building the Price** — before giving the actual number:
   - Tell the client about 2-3 similar projects you did.
   - Give the client the price for THOSE reference projects at **4x-4.5x your cost** (this is the anchor price, not the client's own quote).
   - Do this **10 minutes before** giving the client's actual price.

9. **Giving the Price** — the closing sequence:
   - Tell the client what you're going to do.
   - Ask if they have any questions.
   - Answer any questions.
   - Recap what you're going to do.
   - THEN give the price.
   - Shut up and don't talk — let the client speak first after hearing the price.
   - The actual price given to the client is **3x-3.5x your cost** (lower than the 4-4.5x anchor from step 8 — the anchor makes the real price feel like a deal).
   - Never give a round number.
   - If the client says no: tell them "Not a problem, the price is good [for] 90 days" — keeps the door open without pressure.
   - **Wait 10 minutes before starting rebuttals/loops** (boxed instruction at the bottom of the whiteboard, bridging into the Closing module).

## What he says to do

- If the client wants the price immediately (skips small talk) → skip ahead and start with Measurements (step 3) rather than forcing Connection first.
- After stating the price → say nothing; let the client be the first to speak.
- If the client hesitates on price → do not immediately drop the price; wait 10 minutes before deploying rebuttals/loops (see 07-closing.md for the Closing/Rebuttals/Loops list).
- If the client flatly declines → "Not a problem, the price is good 90 days."
