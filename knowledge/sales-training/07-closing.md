## What he says to do (the 6 closing tools)

1. **Exit / Bathroom / Phone** — a stalling/reset tactic: if the close stalls, use a bathroom break or phone call as a natural pause point [exact mechanic of how this is used to close was not detailed on the whiteboard — likely gives both salesman and client a moment to reset before re-engaging, unverified beyond the label itself].

2. **Company Gift Card** — offering a gift card as an incentive/concession to move a stalled close forward.

3. **Front Yard Sign / Before-and-After Photos / Video Testimonial / Good Review** — social-proof tools: offering to put a yard sign up (often for a discount), and/or showing before/after photos, video testimonials, or reviews to build final confidence.

4. **Benjamin Franklin (Pros/Cons close)** — the classic pros-vs-cons close, laid out on the whiteboard as two columns:
   - **Pros** (framed as yes/no questions to the client): "You like me?" / "You like the company?" / "You like the product?" / "You like the fact that we are a licensed, bonded, and insured company?"
   - **Cons**: "Do you like the price?" — deliberately the ONLY item on the cons side, isolating price as the single objection against a wall of "yes" answers on the pros side.

5. **Good Cop / Bad Cop** — a two-role negotiation tactic (presumably one person — salesman or manager — plays sympathetic while another holds firm on price/terms; exact mechanic not detailed on the whiteboard, [unverified] beyond the label).

6. **Car** — [exact meaning not detailed on the whiteboard — possibly a "let me go check with my manager in the car" stalling/authority-escalation tactic, consistent with common in-home-sales playbooks, but this specific interpretation is [unverified] against Josh's own words since the video wasn't accessed this pass].
